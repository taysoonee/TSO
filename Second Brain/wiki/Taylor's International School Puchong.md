This campus is part of [[Taylor's International School]].

## TIS Puchong (TISPC)
- **Launch Date:** First intake of students commenced in January 2015.
- **Financial Baseline (2014 Budget):**

## Update (May 13, 2026): BDRC 2022 Market Insights (TIS Puchong Red Flags)
- **Operational Crisis:** The BDRC 2022 Study highlighted severe operational red flags for **TIS Puchong**. Parent complaints were acutely high regarding instability of teaching staff (41%), poor discipline (36%), and rising fees (32%). Immediate [[Admissions & Operations]] intervention may be required to stabilize Net Promoter Scores (NPS).

## Update (May 13, 2026): TIS Puchong (TISPC) Enrolment & Attrition Analysis
- **Current Enrolment Health:** TISPC currently holds 1,226 active students (1,206 Current + 20 New Intake). The database shows a massive top-of-funnel backlog of 8,028 'Prospective' leads (primarily Year 0), indicating potential database bloat or unoptimized lead nurturing.
- **Demographic Split:** The campus is heavily reliant on the domestic market with 935 Malaysian students (~76%). However, a strong secondary core of East Asian expatriates has formed, led by China (59), South Korea (50), and Hong Kong (42).
- **Attrition Dynamics:** 
    - A total of 111 leavers cited financial hardship or fee termination, strongly validating [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. 
    - Relocation and Return to Home Country accounted for 232 leavers, highlighting ongoing sensitivity to expatriate movement and validating [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Competitor Flow:** Major destination schools for TISPC leavers are [[Tzu Chi International School]] (10 students) and [[Sunway International School]] (10 students), marking them as the primary catchment competitors. Other notable outflow destinations include [[Taylor's International School KL]] (9, internal transfer), Alice Smith (7), and Sri KL (6).

## Intelligence Update (May 14, 2026): TIS Puchong (TISPC) Enrolment Database & Attrition Drivers
- **Current Base & Funnel Dependency:** A database extraction (raw/K12_Inbox/00. Database/data 4.xlsx) reveals a current enrolment base of 1,225 pupils. The admissions funnel is hyper-localized, drawing heavily from nearby SJKC networks (SJKC Shin Cheng/Harcroft, SJKC Han Ming, SJKC Yak Chee) and preschool chains (Real Kids, Q-Dees). This strongly reinforces [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]], where the 10km radius dictates viability.
- **The Bifurcated Attrition Threat:** Analysis of 1,804 historic withdrawals uncovers a dual-pronged risk validating [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. 
    - **Flight to Affordability:** 121 leavers departed due to "Financial Hardship," actively downgrading to budget/NGO-tier schools with [[Tzu Chi International School]] acting as the primary beneficiary.
    - **Flight to Quality:** 82 leavers cited "Parent Dissatisfaction with teaching," aggressively upgrading to premium/hybrid competitors, most notably [[GEMS Metropark]], [[Nexus International School Malaysia]], and King Henry VIII.

## TISPC Strategic & Academic Update (May 2025)

The TISPC Monthly Management Meeting (MMM) report from May 2025 outlines key operational, academic, and infrastructure shifts at the Puchong campus.

### Academic Performance & IGCSE Results (Class of 2024)
*   **Grade Analysis & Comparison:** BK Gan shared a comparison of academic results for TISPC vs. TISKL (Class of 2024 IGCSE):
    *   **A\*–A:** TISPC 48% vs. TISKL 56%
    *   **A\*–B:** TISPC 67% vs. TISKL 77%
    *   **A\*–C:** TISPC 85% vs. TISKL 90%
*   **Average Value Added (VA):** TISPC recorded a VA of -0.503 for the 2024 cycle.
*   **Action Plan:** BK Gan noted the need to improve performance and adjust the IGCSE KPIs. TISPC secondary department implemented a Key Stage 4 (KS4) Action Plan to close learning gaps for Year 11 (pre-exam) and Year 10 students. The secondary department reviewed English as a Second Language (ESL) groups, conducted New Graded Reading Tests (NGRT), and analysed CAT4 data to determine students' readiness for first-language English.
*   **Future Academic Targets (2025–2030):**
    *   **IGCSE A\*–A Target:** 55% (2025), scaling up to 60% by 2029–2030.
    *   **Value Added (VA) Target:** +0.65 across the 2025–2030 forecast window.

### Five Key Strategic Focus Areas (2025)
1.  **Attainment Gap:** Closing gaps on students' targets and attainment.
2.  **EAL & Multilingual Support:** Reviewing EAL and multilingual needs across the school to support enrolment growth. While Primary is managing its EAL/EEP assessments, Secondary has restructured its ESL groups.
3.  **Digital Learning:** Reviewing the digital learning approach. Head of Teaching and Learning (Secondary) Kerrie represents TISPC in the group. TISPC is defining minimum digital learning requirements and embedding digital learning across staff.
4.  **Digital Marketing:** Reviewing digital marketing platforms (including the school website) to increase engagement and enquiries.
5.  **Parent-Student Journey:** Reviewing the parent-student journey, engagement opportunities, and communication channels. Setting up bi-weekly touchpoints starting end of May 2025.

### Student Enrolment & Demographics
*   **Enrolment Health:** As of 22 January 2025, active enrolment stood at 1,405 students (slightly below the budgeted 1,442). Out of 183 paid students (budgeted at 193), 158 started in January, with the remainder starting in February.
*   **Nationality & Demographic Split:**
    *   **Total Malaysians:** 948 (68% of the student body).
    *   **Key Expat Demographics:** 184 Chinese, 54 Japanese, 51 Korean, and 39 Indian students.
*   **Attrition & Enrolment Concerns:** A decline in Indian and Hong Kong students was observed. Focus groups (July 2024) revealed parent concerns regarding school location, a lack of understanding of holistic education, and syllabus options. Some Chinese parents felt the academic environment was too relaxed (low homework/exams), and agents recommended housing far from TISPC. 

### Operations, Systems & Safeguarding
*   **Unifrog Rollout:** Partnering with Unifrog (led by Kerrie, support from Ellis and James) with student data synced via WONDE from iSAMS. The system is expanding from 'Personal Pathways' to a school-wide secondary rollout.
*   **BYOD Policy:** Mandatory Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) rollout planned for Year 6 in 2026 (optional for Year 6 in 2025). The revised policy is under review by Principal Jane Kuok, AT, and PW.
*   **Discipline & Safeguarding:**
    *   **Student Incident:** A Year 10 student assault incident occurred; the student was asked to leave, and the Ministry of Education (MOE) was formally contacted. Principal Jane Kuok and GH addressed all secondary students.
    *   **Care:mvmt Initiatives:** Implementation of CPOMS (Child Protection Online Management System) & disclosure training, First Aid Mental Health training, TEG Safeguarding Audit preparation, and primary/secondary Ocean Week.
*   **Leadership & Staffing:**
    *   The Chief Growth Officer is set to join TSO on 18 February 2025, with TISPC Admissions and Marketing reporting directly to them.
    *   Head of Counselling interviews are in progress (3 candidates).
    *   Secondary Student Council is expanding in 2025 with new committees (Learning, Enrichment, Voice Councils), building on roles like Ambassadors, Cambassadors, INTERACT, and the Yearbook Committee.

### Marketing & Enrolment Campaigns
*   **Parent Referral Programme:** Relaunched on 12 March 2025 with a digitised CRM form, yielding 28 enquiries. Target for 2025 is 48 enrolments.
*   **10th Anniversary Campaign:** Celebrated with promotions including 50% off upfront fees plus an additional 10% waiver, and a 5% sibling tuition fee discount for the second child. Promotional activities include homepage banners, flag lines, staff anniversary jackets, and student blind-box button badges.
*   **Paid Digital Campaigns:** Relaunched Google and Facebook ads, switching digital ad management to GIS paid agency *Admiral* starting May/June 2025 (Creme Digital contract ended 30 May 2025). Ad spend is adjusting from 40% Meta : 60% Google to 50:50, targeting 60:40 later. Relaunched always-on "Book a Tour" ad campaigns leading to Digistorm automated funnels.
*   **Campaign ROI Targets (May–July 2025):**
    *   *PISF (17–18 May 2025):* Target enquiries = 140, target accepted = 7.
    *   *May Open Week (28 April – 29 May 2025):* Target enquiries = 200, target accepted = 60.
    *   *June–July Open Week (16 June – 11 July 2025):* Target enquiries = 150, target accepted = 38.
*   **Trusted Brands Supplement:** TIS won the Readers' Digest Trusted Brand Award again for 2025, with the Board of Governors (BOG) approving a RM15,000 advertisement budget per campus for the April/May issue.

### Premises & Facilities
