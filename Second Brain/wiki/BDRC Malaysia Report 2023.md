# BDRC International Schools Brand Equity & Market Insights Study (Malaysia) 2023

## Executive Summary
This BDRC report (published 20 November 2023 for Taylors Group of Schools) assesses the brand equity and market insights of international schools in Malaysia. The study highlights post-pandemic market changes, including increased demand from Asian expats and a rejuvenated inbound expat market, while also facing pressures such as cost of living and a heavier reliance on local citizens.

## Key Highlights & Trade-offs
- **Parent Demographics:** A shift towards more Asian expats and Western expats in mixed marriages. Many expats intend to stay in Malaysia long-term (36% for >10 years).
- **Payment of Fees:** Employer-paid fees dropped significantly in 2023 to just 12%, with a massive drop in partial/mixed payments, reflecting changing company policies. 
- **School Choice Drivers:** 
    - **Academic Excellence** remains the top priority when parents must make trade-offs, particularly for Upper Secondary.
    - **Life Skills** is stated as equally important, but traded off in favor of academics.
    - Strong demand exists for **Project-based Learning** (71%) and **Vocational Training** (54%).
    - **Stress/Emotional Counselling** is requested by 47% of parents.
- **Brand Equity Drivers:** The highest drivers of recommendation are:
    1. Good culture & social values
    2. Well-kept building, grounds, and facilities
    3. Reputation for academic excellence
    4. Innovative approach to learning
    5. Wide range of ECAs

## Boarding Schools
Demand remains high (around 50% would consider), but it is heavily concentrated within Malaysia itself (62%) due to the continued weakness of the Malaysian Ringgit.

## Advertising & Recruitment
- **Word of Mouth** (57%) and **Internet Search** (42%) are the primary sources for school discovery.
- **Advertising** accounts for 28% of discovery, with Digital and Social Media being the most recalled channels.
- GIS, Nexus, and Taylors schools show strong digital advertising recall.
