# Tanglin Trust School

## Overview
Tanglin Trust School (TTS) represents a major Tier 1 premium international school benchmark within the [[Singapore School Development Framework]]. Delivering a British-based curriculum with an international perspective, the institution consistently maintains an Ofsted BSO 'Outstanding' rating. It operates as a primary competitor for high-net-worth expat demographics and serves as a direct point of comparative intelligence for [[Nexus International School Singapore]].

## Operational & Financial Baselines
- **Student Demographics:** Enrols over 2,680 students, establishing heavy dominance in the premium expatriate tier.
- **Revenue Structure:** Highly reliant on tuition and associated capital fees, with 97% of total cumulative income derived from fee-based revenue streams, which include mandatory Building Funds and Placement Rights. 
- **Reinvestment & CAPEX:** Substantial, ongoing infrastructural investment is required to maintain premium positioning. Historical baselines (e.g., the 2011/12 financial period) evidenced an S$17m capital expenditure allocation heavily weighted towards major facility upgrades, specifically the West Wing extension.

## Academic & ESG Initiatives
- **Infrastructure & Technology:** A first-mover in large-scale technological standardisation, highlighted by extensive iPad pilots designed to benchmark the digital learning environment expected in premium Singapore international education.
- **Curriculum Architecture:** The school's pedagogical framework is dynamically anchored by four core drivers: Academic Challenge and Engagement, Global Awareness and Sense of Community, Self Awareness and Personal Fulfilment, and Skills for Lifelong Learning. 
- **Parent-Teacher Engagement:** Strategic leveraging of the PTA to fund targeted enhancement projects, including the Berrick Performance Hall audio-visual integrations and spotlights. This heavily builds community retention.
- **Sustainability:** Active engagements in ESG frameworks, notably early environmental drives resulting in the recycling of over 14,660kg of community materials.
