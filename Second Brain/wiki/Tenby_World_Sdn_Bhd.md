# Tenby World Sdn. Bhd.

Tenby World Sdn. Bhd. is a competitor in the Malaysian international school market. It operates as part of a larger group, which underwent a significant ownership change in 2018.

## Corporate Structure &amp; Finance

### FY2019
For the year ended 31 August 2019, the company reported significant profit following a strategic divestment.
- **Revenue:** RM 50,061,207
- **Profit:** RM 41,270,254
- **Strategic Note:** The company transferred its national syllabus business to a related party, Tenby Education Sdn. Bhd., for RM 32.76 million. This indicates a strategic restructuring to focus on its international curriculum offerings.
- **Ultimate Holding Company:** Permotio International Learning S.á.r.l. (Luxembourg).

### FY2018 (8-month period)
For the financial period from 1 January to 31 August 2018, the company demonstrated strong profitability.
- **Revenue:** RM 36,696,528
- **Profit:** RM 7,343,701
- **Strategic Note:** In January 2018, the ultimate holding company changed from Yayasan Ekuiti Nasional (a Malaysian government-formed foundation) to Permotio International Learning S.á.r.l. This ownership shift to an international entity is a significant market event, potentially impacting strategy and capitalisation. The company's financial year-end was also changed to align with its new parent.

### FY2016
For the year ended 31 December 2016, the company recorded strong revenue and profit.
- **Revenue:** RM 48,247,111
- **Profit:** RM 7,912,519
- **Strategic Note:** The company was ultimately held by Yayasan Ekuiti Nasional. It fully settled a RM 3 million term loan during this period, improving its financial stability. This financial strength is a key factor in a market sensitive to the pressures of [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Dormant Entities
Tenby Aman Sdn. Bhd., an immediate subsidiary of Tenby Educare Sdn. Bhd., had not commenced business operations as of FYE 2016. This entity may represent a vehicle for future expansion or land banking, a strategy relevant to [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
