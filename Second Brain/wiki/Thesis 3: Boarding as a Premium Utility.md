# Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility

**Mechanism:** Dual-income UHNW/HNWI families and regional (ASEAN) wealth segments increasingly view premium boarding as an essential utility for security and holistic development, not just accommodation.

**Vector:** Track regional wealth shifts, boarding capacity saturation, and competitor investments in pastoral care infrastructure.

## Supporting Evidence
- **UWC South East Asia Pricing (2018/2019):** Demonstrated that top-tier regional competitors can command upwards of SGD 35,610 (approx. RM 124,635) for boarding alone, pushing the combined annual cost for a senior boarder above SGD 82,000 (approx. RM 287,000). This validates the high elasticity and willingness to pay within the premium regional market. See [[UWC South East Asia]].
