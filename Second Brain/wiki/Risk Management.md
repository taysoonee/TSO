# Risk Management

The risk management framework actively tracks and mitigates operational, financial, and strategic risks across Taylor's Schools (TS). The following insights are based on the July 2022 Risk Management Update [[2022 TS Risk-RMC-Updated (280722).pdf]].

## Top Corporate Risks (High Impact / High Likelihood)

### 1. High Student Attrition
- **Causes:** Economic downturn, dissatisfaction with online learning, financial constraints, relocation, and competition.
- **Affected:** AISM, NIS, TIS, GIS.
- **Mitigation:** Re-assess school USPs, offer discounts/scholarships, provide financial assistance (installment plans), and strengthen online/hybrid learning quality. Exception approvals for Leave of Absence (LOA) are also utilized.
- **Link:** This risk directly aligns with the [[Market Intelligence]] mandate to track student attrition and conduct pricing reviews.

### 2. Eroding Profit Margin & Financial Covenants
- **Causes:** Market limits on fee increases, inability to manage costs, and low cash positions resulting in higher net debt.
- **Affected:** All TS (profit margins), PEPL/NISS (CGIF financial covenants).
- **Mitigation:** Strict cost management, optimization of academic staff pay scales (localization strategy), and injection of cash/equity to make up covenant shortfalls.

### 3. Staffing Challenges (Expat and Local)
- **Causes:** Poor recruitment strategies, market competition, currency depreciation (RM), and lack of effective agencies.
- **Affected:** NISS, TISKL, GIS, NISM, TISPC, AISM.
- **Mitigation:** Expanding recruitment sourcing channels, optimizing staffing early, and conducting salary reviews every 3 years to remain competitive.

### 4. IT Infrastructure & Cybersecurity
- **Causes:** Inadequate firewall protection, lack of backup plans, and server hardware failures.
- **Affected:** Primarily NISS and TS Malaysia.
- **Mitigation:** Implementation of DMZ, signing a new 5-year network maintenance contract with Cisco (2022-2027), and upgrading server rooms with generators and temperature monitoring.

### 5. Health & Safety: Infectious Diseases and Student Welfare
- **Context:** Operations face constant risk of school closures or reputational damage from outbreaks like Hand Foot and Mouth Disease (HFMD), Dengue, and Food Poisoning. 
- **Mitigation/Opportunities:** Reviewing insurance benchmarks against retail products like the AIA Flex PA (which explicitly covers 23 infectious diseases including HFMD and Dengue, plus food poisoning and a "Child Plus" education allowance rider). Leveraging Taylor's existing relationship with AIA (see [[TEG Benefits]]) could present opportunities to negotiate comprehensive Group Student PA plans to transfer financial risks associated with outbreaks or parent/student accidents.
