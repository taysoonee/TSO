# Australian International School Malaysia (AISM)

Australian International School Malaysia (AISM) is a premium international school within the Taylor's Education Group portfolio. It is classified as a Tier 1 asset in the [[Competitor Analysis]].

## Admissions Data
Based on a Term 4 2025/2026 Year 5 offer letter, key admission and financial details include:
- **Application Fee:** RM 1,300.00 (Observed waiver: 100% rebate through the "Book A Tour Promo").
- **Admission Fee:** RM 20,000.00 (Observed waiver: 50% waiver through the "Book A Tour Promo", reducing it to RM 10,000.00).
- **Tuition Fee:** RM 20,660.00 (Base tuition for Year 5, excluding additional service taxes or discounts).
- **Discounts:** A 5% Sibling Discount is applicable to tuition fees.
- **Refundable Deposit:** RM 18,736.00, subject to the Terms and Conditions Governing Enrolment and Admissions.

**Source:** [[AISM offer letter.pdf]]

## Update (May 13, 2026): Enrolment Pipeline & Demographics Intelligence
Analysis of the master AISM database (12,682 historical records) reveals critical shifts in the post-pandemic funnel and parent demographics:
- **Pipeline & Recovery:** The school maintains a massive top-of-funnel (9,111 historically Prospective students). There is a confirmed post-pandemic recovery trend: new entries accelerated from 69 (2020) to 131 (2022). At the same time, attrition slowed from 152 leavers (2020) to 111 (2022), resulting in a net positive enrolment shift in 2022 (+20 students). This partially mitigates the severe 34% contraction observed between 2019 and 2021. Linked to [[Admissions & Operations]].
- **Domestic Shift & Inflation:** Despite the Australian curriculum, the student base is overwhelmingly domestic (5,274 Malaysians vs. 828 Australians and 705 Chinese). Between 2020-2022, "Financial Hardship" caused 28 withdrawals, validating [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].
- **Expatriate Volatility:** The expatriate market remains highly elastic. Between 2020-2022, 152 withdrawals were directly attributed to "Return to home country" and "Relocation," validating [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Curriculum Vulnerability:** While AISM previously captured students seeking different curricula, 22 internal leavers (2020-2022) cited "Not the preferred curriculum" as their departure reason, indicating potential leakage to UK/IB competitors. 

## Update (May 13, 2026): Financial Squeeze & Occupancy Crash (2016-2021)
- **EBITDA Contraction:** AISM financial performance deteriorated significantly, transitioning from a positive EBITDA of RM3.8m in 2016 to a negative RM4.5m by 2021. PBT correspondingly sank from RM2.0m to -RM6.2m.
- **Enrolment & Occupancy Squeeze:** EFTS contracted sharply from 632 to just 396 students over the same period. This drastic reduction caused campus occupancy rates to plummet from 70% to an unsustainable 42%. Teaching occupancy also declined from 79% to 63%, indicating a lag in right-sizing faculty against enrolment drops.
- **Discounting Tactics:** In response to the contracting pipeline, scholarship and bursary usage as a % of Gross Revenue more than tripled from 1.4% (2016) to 4.4% (2021), reflecting aggressive, margin-eroding price promotions to defend market share.
- **Strategic Implication:** The heavy reliance on discounting combined with plummeting occupancy and negative EBITDA highlights the acute vulnerability of the premium tier, directly driven by the expatriate outflow and domestic price sensitivity. Linked to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Regional Competitor Benchmarking: AISS Arbitrage
A major strategic opportunity exists when benchmarking AISM against its Singaporean counterpart, [[Australian International School Singapore]] (AISS). Based on the AISS 2023 fee schedule, Senior Secondary education in Singapore costs upwards of SGD 47,140/year (~MYR 162,600) with an additional ~SGD 10,000 (~MYR 35,000) just in entry fees.

By integrating [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]] and [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]], AISM is perfectly positioned to capture regional expatriate families seeking top-tier Australian curriculum. AISM offers an identical curriculum trajectory but at a significantly lower total cost of ownership, even when factoring in premium boarding infrastructure.
