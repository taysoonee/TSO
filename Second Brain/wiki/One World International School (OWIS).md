# One World International School (OWIS)

## Market Positioning & 2018 Milestone
- **Context:** OWIS fundamentally disrupted the [[Singapore K-12 Market]] in 2018 when they won the Economic Development Board (EDB) tender for an "affordable foreign school system."
- **Capacity:** Their Nanyang campus opened with a 1,200-seat capacity, driving the mid-market competitive shift.
- **Pricing Disruption:** Positioned squarely in the affordable tier, charging SGD 17,000 – SGD 20,000 (approx. MYR 59,500 – MYR 70,000) against the SGD 45,000+ charged by Tier-1 operators. 
- **Strategy:** They utilize a 30% nationality cap to prevent a "local-schooling" feel, maintaining diversity while keeping overheads low. This approach aligns heavily with catering to "Local-Plus" expat packages (linking to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]).

## Update (May 14, 2026): Mid-Priced Expansion Model
- **Capital Injection:** OWIS invested over SGD 10 million to launch its 1,200 capacity campus in 2018.
- **Curriculum:** Unlike other ultra-lean mid-priced competitors (like Invictus or Middleton), OWIS differentiated by offering comprehensive IB and IGCSE programs within the "affordable" tier (SGD 17,000 - 20,000 / ~MYR 52,530 - 61,800).
- **Competitive Context:** This launch occurred amid a structurally oversupplied market (Schools CAGR 11.4% vs Enrolment 7.1% as of Feb 2017), allowing OWIS to systematically capture price-sensitive expatriates migrating from underfilled Tier 1 premium schools.

## Pricing Shift: Academic Year 2019-2020
- **Application Fee:** SGD 1,030 (approx. MYR 3,605) + 7% GST.
- **Early Childhood to Grade 6:** SGD 17,514 (approx. MYR 61,299).
- **Grades 7 to 10:** SGD 20,136 (approx. MYR 70,476).
- **Strategic Note:** The fee model is heavily 'inclusive,' bundling uniforms (2 sets), academic field trips, textbooks, and IT equipment. This 'all-in' pricing tactic avoids nickel-and-diming parents, directly appealing to expats with leaner relocation packages and families facing the regional equivalent of the [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. This mid-tier pricing provides a competitive benchmark against Taylor's Schools' regional pricing strategies.
- **Source:** [[OWIS - 2019.pdf]]
