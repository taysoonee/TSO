# Singapore Land Development & Public Communication

## Regulatory Framework: Public Communications Plan (EDB)
New school developments or expansions on land parcels in Singapore (often via Economic Development Board - EDB Request for Interest/Tender) are subject to a mandatory **Public Communications Plan**. This plan ensures the local community is informed and feedback is addressed prior to key planning approvals.

### Local Community Definition
The local community is defined as stakeholders within an approximate **100m radius** of the Land Parcel, including:
- Residents of HDB flats, private condominiums, and landed houses.
- Management Corporation Strata Title (MCST) Committees and Neighbourhood Committees.
- Administration of nearby schools and institutions.
- Local Member of Parliament (MP), Constituency Director, and Town Council General Manager.

### Two-Stage Notification Process

#### Stage 1: Prior to Application for Written Permission
- **Action:** Distribute flyers containing project information (type, height, access), location map, infrastructure works, and construction milestones.
- **Requirement:** Must occur prior to hoarding erection, site clearance, or tree-felling.
- **Feedback:** Flyers must include contact details for community feedback and hotline numbers for BCA, NEA, MOM, and EDB.
- **Compliance:** Successful Tenderer must submit **Form A** (Plan) and **Form B** (Declaration of distribution) to the Authority (EDB) to receive written consent for the development application.

#### Stage 2: Prior to Resubmission (Post-Provisional Permission)
- **Action:** Distribute additional detailed flyers including schematic site layouts.
- **Timeline:** Community must be given at least **2 weeks** to respond to the proposal.
- **Compliance:** Submit **Form C** (Declaration & preliminary feedback collation) and **Form D** (Final collation and mitigation measures).
- **Structural Works:** Structural works cannot commence until EDB gives written consent or Written Permission is granted under the Planning Act.

### Strategic Implications for Taylor's Schools
- **Regulatory Friction:** The mandatory 2-stage notification and feedback collation process introduces significant lead time and public scrutiny for any Singapore greenfield or brownfield expansion.
- **Community Sensitivity:** Proposed developments must "sensitively address" community concerns (e.g., noise, dust, vehicular access) to gain EDB consent for planning resubmissions.
- **Inter-Agency Coordination:** Developers must coordinate with BCA, NEA, MOM, and URA throughout the process.
