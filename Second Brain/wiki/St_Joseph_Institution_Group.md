# St. Joseph's Institution (SJII) Group

## Singapore Operations (SJII & SJII Elementary)
The Singapore divisions act as a hyper-profitable nucleus for the broader brand. In FY 2018, SJI International Ltd captured S$ 38 million in revenue (delivering a S$ 5.57 million surplus), while the Elementary wing secured S$ 21.35 million (producing an additional S$ 3.93 million surplus). 
*Strategic Imperative:* SJII operates against a hard external countdown—their core premise leases expire in December 2025. Consequently, their massive operating surpluses (totalling unrestricted funds near S$ 28.7M) are aggressively sequestered into development reserves earmarked for acquiring and constructing a post-2025 mega-campus. 

## Malaysian Expansion Pivot (Tropicana SJII)
By sharp contrast, their Malaysian franchise deployment, Tropicana SJII Education Management Sdn Bhd, encountered immense establishment friction. During its formative scaling (2014-2017), the Malaysian entity ran persistent accumulated tax losses. Despite generating RM 29 million in revenue by 2017, the school posted a severe net loss of RM 9.8 million, driven overwhelmingly by burdensome rental expenditures and start-up inertia. This stark dichotomy in operating margins validates the severe local constraints mapped in [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
