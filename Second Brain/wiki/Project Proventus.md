Project Proventus is a long-term strategic initiative within Taylor's Schools.

## M&A Sandbox & Vietnam Expansion (March 2023)
The M&A Sandbox under Project Proventus explicitly prioritizes Vietnam, Singapore, and Malaysia, seeking minimum 15% IRR and S$5m EBITDA. 

### Vietnam Market Focus
- **Curriculum:** Strong focus on premium bilingual schools in Vietnam.
- **Ecopark Campus Opportunity:** Evaluating a 50/50 joint venture to launch a potential "Nexus Ecopark" campus in Hanoi (capacity 1,500-2,000 students). Requires mitigating near-term expat demographic risks.
- **APU (Da Nang):** Actively evaluating the K-12 and University campus. APU generated c. USD 9m EBITDA with high popularity of the US curriculum in Vietnam.

### Supplementary Learning
Exploring vertical integration into pre-schools and enrichment centres in Vietnam, such as Language Link Vietnam (LLV), to funnel into core K-12 programs. Links to [[Supplementary Learning Strategy]].

## Update (November 2021)
During the "Regaining our Growth Momentum" (SpA 2021) presentation, a major update regarding Project Proventus was shared, anticipating the announcement of a new minority investor by November 2021. This move aligns with broader executive strategies to capitalize on post-COVID market dynamics, including potential M&A opportunities with distressed schools.

### M&A Sandbox Updates (March 2023)
- **Project Orchid:** The sandbox explicitly considers "Project Orchid", which involves the potential relocation of the Nexus campus and the rebranding of the Putrajaya campus as [[Taylor's International School]] (TIS).
- **AISM Conversion:** The committee also noted the potential strategic conversion of [[Australian International School Malaysia]] to the TIS brand, depending on underlying catchment dynamics and feasibility.
Source: [[M&A Committee meeting 20230330.pdf]]

## Update (May 14, 2026): Proventus Financial & Real Estate Data
- **Financial Baseline (2011-2020):** Data extracted for Project Proventus (likely benchmarking GIS or the broader premium portfolio) indicates a strategic yield transition. Despite enrolment dropping from a peak of 2,136 (2014) to 1,872 (2020), Revenue grew from RM 118m to RM 143.3m, and EBITDA expanded from RM 32.8m to RM 48.4m. This aligns with a strategy of protecting yield and premium brand positioning despite volume loss (links to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]).
- **Competitor Real Estate Models:** The Proventus data maps competitor operating models against developer partnerships. Premium (KH8, Peninsula) and Budget (Kingsley) campuses are largely "Developer owned and operated". Mid-Price competitors (GEMS Metropark, Tenby Setia Eco Park, Tenby Tropicana Aman) favor "Renting from developer". This real estate matrix directly feeds into [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]] for evaluating new campus feasibility.
