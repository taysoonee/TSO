# Canadian International School (Singapore)

## Overview
Canadian International School (CIS) is a premium international school operating in Singapore, offering the IB curriculum.

## Update (May 14, 2026): 2018-2019 Fee Strategy
- **Segmented Pricing:** Market intelligence from 2018-2019 reveals that CIS employed a divergent pricing strategy between its campuses. The **Lakeside Campus** implemented a modest +2% fee increase, while the **TK Campus** saw a -2% fee reduction.
- **Strategic Impact:** This indicates a targeted response to localized demographics and competitive pressure, unlike the massive double-digit hikes seen at Tier-1 peers (SAIS +13%, AISS +10%). This hyper-local pricing adjustment aligns with the dynamics discussed in [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
- **Source:** [[NISS v2.pptx]]


## Pricing Shift: 2022/2023 Lakeside Campus
- **Tuition & Premiums:** English Programme fees scale from S$33,950 (~MYR 104,905) for Pre-K to Grade 3, up to S$44,200 (~MYR 136,578) for Grades 11-12.
- **Bilingual Strategy & FDI Capture:** The Chinese-English Bilingual Programme is aggressively priced at a premium over the standard English track (e.g., Grades 4-6 is S$41,550 vs S$37,900). This indicates a strong willingness to pay among the Chinese expatriate demographic and supports the regional observations in [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Retention Tactic:** CIS explicitly absorbed the Singapore government's 1% GST hike (7% to 8% in January 2023) for Semester 2, using tax absorption as a localized retention and affordability mechanism.
- **Source:** [[CIS LK - 2022.pdf]]

## Pricing Shift (2019/2020)
- **Fee Increase:** CIS introduced a +1.5% fee increase for the 19/20 academic year.
- **Published Rates (19/20):** Senior year fees (Y12-Y13) reached SGD 40,900 (approx. RM 143,150). This positions CIS slightly below the Tier 1 pricing of [[Nexus International School Singapore]] within the competitive [[Singapore K-12 Market]].

## Historical Baseline: 2015/2016 Fee Schedule
- **Tuition Benchmarks:** Lakeside Campus ranged from S$17,712 (~MYR 54,696) for Pre-K Half Day up to S$36,600 (~MYR 113,024) for Grades 11-12. Tanjong Katong Campus was priced slightly lower, peaking at S$33,940 (~MYR 104,810) for Grade 4 Bilingual. 
- **Barrier to Entry:** Non-refundable Application Fee of S$2,140 (~MYR 6,608) and a Confirmation Fee of S$3,200 (~MYR 9,882) for the first child. The confirmation fee was waived for the third child onwards, incentivizing larger expat families.
- **Strategic Impact:** Comparing this 2015/2016 baseline to the 2022/2023 fees (where Grades 11-12 reached S$44,200), CIS exhibited a ~20% fee growth over 7 years. This historical context highlights the escalating cost of premium education in Singapore, reinforcing [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]] as Taylor's Schools can position its Malaysian K-12 boarding options as a highly compelling, cost-effective alternative for regional expats.
- **Source:** [[CIS - 2015.pdf]]

## Pricing Shift (2018/19 Split Strategy)
In the 2018/19 cycle, [[Canadian International School (Singapore)]] implemented a deliberate split-campus pricing strategy. The main Lakeside campus saw a +2% fee increase, bringing its top year fee to SGD 39,940 (~RM 139,790). Conversely, the Tanjong Katong (TK) campus fees were reduced by -2%, capping its top year fee at SGD 34,100 (~RM 119,350). This targeted approach highlights a method for managing capacity and capturing cost-conscious segments via secondary campuses. Source: [[NISS v1.pptx]]
