## Update (May 14, 2026): Launch Discount Strategy & Pricing Model
- **Pricing Strategy:** The school launched with a flat Standard Rate of SGD 25,000 (approx. MYR 77,250) for Primary Years.
- **Tapering Discounts:** To aggressively acquire market share, they employed a tapering discount strategy: 30% discount in 2020-21, 15% in 2021-22, and 10% in 2022-23.
- **Strategic Impact:** This aggressive entry pricing directly targeted the 'Affordable Tier' and placed significant downward pressure on mid-market competitors, validating regional price sensitivity described in [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Update (May 14, 2026): Chip Eng Seng Backing and Rapid Scaling
- **Corporate Synergy:** The Perse School Singapore's development is driven by a partnership between Cambridge International and Chip Eng Seng (CES Cambridge), demonstrating property developer influx into the K-12 sector.
- **Academic Expansion:** After a strategic relaunch in Jan 2025 at its Upper Bukit Timah campus, the school is accelerating its secondary pathway, scheduled to open Year 11 by August 2026. This transitions it to a full 3-18 pipeline.
- **Positioning:** Operating in the SGD 25k-27k mid-range, The Perse markets itself as "Affordable British Excellence", leveraging its prestige brand to capture the inflation-squeezed expat demographic. This poses a direct threat to [[Nexus International School Singapore]] in the Bukit Timah corridor.

## Update (May 14, 2026): Primary Year Discounting Strategy
- **Pricing Model:** To aggressively capture early market share, [[The Perse School Singapore]] established a flat standard rate of SGD 25,000 (approx. MYR 87,500) strictly for its Primary Year offering.
- **Sliding-Scale Discounting:** They deployed a massive, transparent cohort-building discount structure: 30% discount in 2020 (effective fee ~MYR 61,250), 15% discount in 2021 (~MYR 74,375), and 10% discount in 2022 (~MYR 78,750).
- **Strategic Impact:** This aggressive 'buy-in' tactic targets price-sensitive expatriates and forces legacy premium players to either match introductory pricing or accept top-of-funnel leakage. This validates regional pricing warfare dynamics tied to [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Update (May 14, 2026): Relaunch & Ownership Shift (2025)
- **Corporate Restructuring:** Following its aggressive 2020-2022 discounting strategy (which included 30% first-year discounts and 10% 'lifetime' early-bird discounts), the school underwent an ownership shift. It relaunched in January 2025 under **Futuris Education Pte Ltd** at a new Upper Bukit Timah campus.
- **Pricing Continuity:** Despite the change in ownership, the school maintained its mid-tier pricing strategy, with current fees for Nursery to Year 6 sitting at SGD 25,641 (approx. MYR 89,743). 
- **Strategic Implication:** The ownership volatility suggests the initial heavy discounting model to build the 2020 cohort may have faced sustainability challenges. This provides [[Taylor's Schools]] with a critical counter-narrative opportunity: emphasizing institutional legacy and financial stability against 'boutique' entrants. Validates monitoring of mid-tier sustainability under [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
- **Source:** Grounded Web Search Verification (May 2026).
