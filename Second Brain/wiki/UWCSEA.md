# UWC South East Asia (UWCSEA)

## Core Intelligence (2016/2017 Baseline)
- **Jurisdiction:** Singapore (Dover & East campuses)
- **Demographic:** Largest international school in Singapore, 5,529 students.
- **Financials:** Revenue SGD 190.7M, Profit SGD 11.9M. High reliance on tuition fees (72-75%).
- **Academics:** 36.7 average IB diploma score (98.4% pass rate).

## Strategic Operations & ESG
Like our own portfolio-wide [[ESG|Solar PV Integration]] rollout to hedge against [[Thesis 2: The Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]], UWCSEA has heavily invested in sustainable development, including comprehensive solar and green infrastructure, validating this as a regional standard.

## Vulnerabilities & Academic Sentiment
Despite a high IB average, the 2016/2017 parent survey revealed a Net Promoter Score (NPS) drop to 45%. Significantly, **50.6% of students receive academic tuition outside of school**, and 48% of parents cited insufficient in-school support as a primary concern. This highlights a critical branding weakness in the regional premium tier that Taylor's Schools can exploit by emphasising comprehensive, internally resourced academic support.

## Alternative Revenue Streams & Philanthropy
UWCSEA operates a highly advanced alumni relations programme (established 2006) with over 12,000 contactable members. They allocate 3.5% of standard school fees towards their scholarship programme (supporting 110 scholars). Furthermore, they operate sophisticated tiered giving structures, including the **1971 Society** (for major donors/giving circles) and the **Kurt Hahn Society** (dedicated to legacy/bequest planning). This structure yields strong engagement, raising over SGD 590,000 with a 21% alumni giving rate.
