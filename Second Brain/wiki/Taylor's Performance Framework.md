# Taylor's Performance Framework (TPF)

The Taylor's Performance Framework (TPF) is the standardized performance management system for [[Taylor's Education Group]] non-academic staff, managed via the **ePerformance** platform (built on [[Cornerstone]]).

## Framework Components
As of the 2015 baseline:
- **eGoals (60%):** Individual performance goals set using the **SMART** (Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound) approach.
- **Core Competencies (40%):** 10 competencies categorized under Strategy, Communication, People, and Execution.
- **Core Values (R.E.C.I.P.E.):** Acting as an overriding factor in performance ratings.

## Performance Cycle
- **October:** Goal setting between employees and managers.
- **Ongoing:** Continuous feedback and two-way dialogue.
- **September/October:** Annual Year-End Review and senior management moderation.
- **December:** Performance Bonus payout.
- **January:** Annual Increment and Promotions take effect.

## Rating Scale
A 5-point scale is utilized for appraisals:
1. **Outstanding**
2. **Excellent**
3. **Competent**
4. **Marginal**
5. **Unsatisfactory**

## Implementation Roadmap
- **2013:** Managerial & above (Job Grades M11/M12, S13-15, T16-17).
- **2014:** Inclusion of M10.
- **2015:** Inclusion of Professional and Individual Contributors (Job Grades P5-P7, IC10-13).

## Update (May 12, 2026): Transition to SAP SuccessFactors
- **Platform Migration:** The performance appraisal system has transitioned to **SAP SuccessFactors** for the 2025 review cycle.
- **Appraisal Structure (2025):**
    - **Goals/KPIs:** 60% weightage.
    - **Core Competencies:** 40% weightage.
    - **Core Values (R.E.C.I.P.E.):** Continues as an overriding decision factor.
- **Key Competencies Tracked:** Communicating Effectively, Creative Thinking & Innovation, Customer & Service Orientation, Managing & Developing People, Planning & Organising, Problem Solving & Decision Making, Relationship Building & Networking, Results Orientation, Strategic Orientation & Leadership, Teamwork & Collaboration.
- **Source:** [[2025 Taylor's Schools Annual Performance Review (Non Academic) for Tay Soon Ee - SAP SuccessFactors.pdf]]

## 2014 System Baseline (eReview)
- **Primary Tool:** Cornerstone on Demand (ePerformance).
- **Implementation Status:** Standardized for Grade M10 and above by Sept 2014.
- **Workflow:** Fully digitized process including self-review, manager review, and senior leadership moderation, eliminating manual sign-offs.
- **Framework Components:** Confirmed use of R.E.C.I.P.E. Core Values and the 10 Core Competencies model (behavioral indicators).
- **Source:** [[ePerformance (eREVIEW - Employee Task) - 5Sept14.pdf]]

## Historical Performance Framework (2014)
- **Structure:** Managers (M11/M12) were appraised on Core Values (60%), Managerial Expectations (30%), and Annual Priorities/KPIs (40% weighting logic applied to key areas).
- **Core Values:** Respect/Care, Culture of Excellence, Open Communication, Integrity, Passion, and Enjoyable Environments.

## Historical Strategic Planning Framework (2010-2011)
- **Primary Tool:** Power Planner Template (XLS).
- **Framework:** Included a **1-Page Strategic Planner** and **Tactical Action Plans**.
- **Core Components:** 
    - **Core Ideology:** Core Values and Purpose.
    - **BHAG:** Big Hairy Audacious Goal.
    - **Brand Promise:** Key value proposition.
    - **SWOT Analysis:** Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
- **Execution Model:** Quarterly tactical action plans with weekly tracking (W14-W52) of objectives and KPIs. This indicates a high level of accountability and structured review long before the migration to Cornerstone/SuccessFactors.
- **Source:** [[2010 Power Planner - template.xls]]

*Note: The 2010 Power Planner framework serves as the precursor to the current [[Meeting Cadence]] and [[Taylor's Performance Framework]].*
