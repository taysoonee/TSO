## Update (May 13, 2026): Enrolment & Attrition Analysis (Sep 2021 Data)
A comprehensive review of the September 2021 GIS database (2109 GIS.xlsx) revealed the following strategic insights:
- **Demographic Split:** Current enrolment (1,579 active/new pupils) is approximately 40% domestic (Malaysian) and 60% expatriate. The top expatriate cohorts are from India, the UK, South Korea, and China.
- **Expatriate Volatility:** High leaver volumes in 2020-2021 cited "Return to Home Country" and "Relocation" (~182-205 annually). This structural dependence on the expat demographic firmly validates [[The Living Ledger]]'s [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **The Domestic Squeeze:** Students leaving GIS for "Financial Reason" are primarily downgrading to affordable-tier competitors, with [[Taylor's International School Kuala Lumpur|Taylor's International School KL]] absorbing the largest share, followed by Sri KDU and [[Cempaka International School]]. This confirms [[The Living Ledger]]'s [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].
- **Curriculum Attrition:** Withdrawals due to "Prefer Different Curriculum" predominantly move to [[Project ISKL Campus Bid]] (ISKL) and [[Australian International School Malaysia]] (AISM). This highlights a vulnerability for GIS against American/IB and Australian curricula.
- **Top Feeder Preschools:** The primary feeders into GIS (2019-2021) are The Children's House and Children's Discovery House. Defensively, GIS successfully acquired students from Sri KDU and St Joseph's Institution International School.

## Update (May 13, 2026): Financial Performance & Occupancy Yield (2016-2021)
- **EBITDA Compression:** GIS experienced gradual EBITDA compression from RM45.7m in 2016 to RM41.1m in 2021, with PBT declining from RM43.1m to RM35.8m. Despite the decline, GIS remains the largest cash generator in the portfolio, consistently providing >RM50m in available cash annually.
- **Enrolment Contraction:** EFTS steadily dropped from a peak of 2,123 (2016) to 1,671 (2021), driving the campus occupancy rate down from 88% to 73%. New enrolments nearly halved from 444 to 265 over the 5-year period.
- **Discounting Restraint:** Unlike AISM, GIS maintained strict discounting discipline, with scholarships remaining extremely low (0.7% to 1.2% of Gross Revenue). This indicates strong brand equity and pricing power, despite volume drops.
- **Strategic Implication:** The contraction in EFTS and occupancy without an accompanying spike in discounting confirms that GIS is strategically absorbing volume losses (likely tied to the expat exodus - [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]) to protect yield and brand premium, rather than engaging in a price war.
