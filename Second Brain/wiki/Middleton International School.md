# Middleton International School

## Market Positioning & Lean Operation (2019 Landscape)
- **Context:** Operated by EtonHouse, Middleton is a key player in the "Mid-Priced" disruption of the [[Singapore K-12 Market]], directly targeting expatriates on local packages (which exclude housing and school fee allowances).
- **Pricing:** Annual fees structured around SGD 19,299 (approx. MYR 59,634).
- **Lean Operational Model:** 
  - Focuses on primary cohorts first.
  - Maintains a 28-student class size to maximize yield per teacher.
  - Minimal facilities, providing only a play field, significantly reducing CapEx and OpEx.
  - Capacity: 135.
- **Pipeline Strategy:** Graduates are designed to seamlessly transition to EtonHouse Secondary School (Broadrick Road), effectively using the low-cost Middleton campus as an early-years funnel for the premium network.
- **Strategic Impact:** Middleton's success highlights the vulnerability of Tier-1 schools to lean disruptors and validates the growing demand for "value-for-money" options among modern expats, directly supporting the market constraints tracked in [[The Living Ledger|Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].

## 2017-18 Fee Positioning & Market Disruption
- **Affordable Tier Pricing:** Taylor's Schools tracked Middleton (MPS) as a key disruptive player in the mid-priced segment for the 2017-18 academic year, with Pre-K fees at SGD 15,000 and an average fee structure of SGD 17,615.
- **Strategic Impact:** This aggressive pricing structure placed Middleton significantly below premium competitors like [[Nexus International School Singapore]] (Pre-K SGD 21,030), capturing the growing "Local-Plus" and self-paying expat demographic in Singapore.
