# Epsom College Malaysia

## Overview
Epsom College in Malaysia is a direct Tier 1 competitor, heavily focused on boarding and premium education. It serves as a key indicator for both domestic and expatriate market dynamics.

## Demographics & Foreign Demand (2023 Update)
As of 2023, the school has robust foreign demand, heavily dependent on China, which accounts for 47% of applications. The cohort sits at roughly 50% Malaysian, 18% Chinese, 17% Japanese, and 12% Korean. The school is on track for rapid enrolment growth (500+ students) under a renewed strategic focus.

## Strategic Shifts & Expansion (2023 Update)
- **Boarding Expansion:** The school is aggressively expanding its boarding capacity, with new houses opening in 2024. 
- **Domestic Targeting:** In a direct move against competitors, Epsom introduced a "Weekday Boarding" package (Monday to Friday) equipped with transport. This specifically targets local Malaysian families, increasing competition for our boarding operations.
- **Sports Academies:** Significant CapEx investment into specialized academies including Mouratoglou Tennis, La Liga Football, and Golf, reinforcing their premium positioning.

## Update (May 14, 2026): Aggressive Fee Tactics & Market Capture
- **Market Share Capture:** Extracted intelligence reveals that Epsom successfully captured students directly from Mont' Kiara International School (MKIS) by leveraging aggressive scholarships and offering a 3-year fee lock guarantee. 
- **Capacity Scale:** Epsom has deployed massive boarding infrastructure, building capacity for up to 800 boarders. This scale validates [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]] and positions them as a dominant player in the regional premium boarding market.

## FY2021 Financial Distress
- **Financial Impact:** Revenue dropped by 17.8% YoY to RM 25.5m. The school registered a massive RM -18.9m PBT loss, signaling severe operational distress.
- **Strategic Implication:** This extreme vulnerability may force desperate [[Marketing & Enquiries]] fee promotions or position them as a prime distressed asset in the K-12 market.
