# Historical Pivot: Local Student Quota Abolishment (2012)

## Overview
In 2012, the Malaysian government abolished the 40% local student quota for international schools. This policy shift allowed international schools to admit an unlimited number of Malaysian students, fundamentally transforming the market landscape.

## Impact on Tier-1 Competitors
- **[[The British School of Kuala Lumpur (BSKL)]]:** Saw a massive 81% revenue surge in FY2012 (RM 24.68m) as it aggressively captured domestic demand.
- **Strategic Shift:** Schools previously restricted by quotas moved from expatriate-heavy models to domestic-dominant models, supporting the long-term growth of brands like [[Taylor's International School]].
