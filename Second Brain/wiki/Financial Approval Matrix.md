# Financial Approval Matrix

The Financial Approval Matrix (FAM) governs the delegation of financial authority within Taylor's Education Group (TEG) [[Approval matrix v7_Nov2018.pdf]].

## General Principles
- **No Splitting:** Transactions must not be intentionally split to evade approval limits.
- **Endorsement:** Expenditures > RM10,000 (or SGD 5,000) require endorsement by the respective Finance Manager.
- **Unbudgeted Items:** Unbudgeted items > RM30,000 require GCFO endorsement; > RM500,000 require Board notification.

## Re-delegation
- Financial authority can be re-delegated temporarily or permanently via email notification to Central Purchasing Department (CPD) and the Finance Manager.

## Strategic & Operations Approval Matrix (School Principals)
The Strategic and Operations Approval Matrix for Principals (Rev 11, effective Sept 2013) provides specific authority levels for school-level leadership [[TS - Strategic and Operations Approval Matrix Rev11  (Final).pdf]].

### Key Approval Authorities (A = Approval, E = Endorse, C = Consult)
| Matter | Approval Authority |
| :--- | :--- |
| 10-Year Blueprint | Board of Governors (BoG) - Endorsed by EXCO |
| Curriculum Changes (e.g. IB) | Taylor's Schools Office (TSO) President |
| School Fee Policy Changes | Board of Governors (BoG) |
| Salary Scale Changes | Board of Governors (BoG) - Endorsed by EXCO |
| Compensation & Benefits | Board of Governors (BoG) - Endorsed by EXCO |
| Tenders > RM50,000 | TSO President |
| Unbudgeted Purchases | Varies (Refer to Capex Approval Matrix) |

### Functional Specialists (Consultation)
Principals are required to consult with TSO Specialists in the following areas:
- **Finance (Fin):** School annual budgets, fee policies.
- **Human Resources (HR):** Recruitment, salary scales, benefits.
- **ICT:** Strategy and system implementation.
- **Legal & Government (L&G):** Contracts, trademarks, school fee recovery.

## Linkage to Proposal Standards
All capital expenditures (Capex) or operational shifts (Opex) exceeding local authority limits must be accompanied by a standard [[Investment Committee]] **Information Pack**.

### Key Insights from Rev 11 Matrix Updates
- **[EXECUTIVE]** Changes to 10-Year Blueprint require TEG EXCO (Endorse), BOG (Approve), MMM (Consult), and Principal (Recommend).
- **[MARKETING]** Special discount schemes and scholarship changes require BOG (Approve), MMM (Consult), Principal (Recommend), and TSO Specialist Fin (Consult). Development of promotional materials requires Principal (Recommend), Brand Custodian (Consult), and TSO Specialist Mkt (Consult).
- **[ADMISSIONS]** Changes to admissions policies and guidelines (e.g., entrance assessments) require variable Approval by BOD/EXCO/BOG based on impact, Recommendation by Principal, and Consultation with Legal.

## v7 Specific Amendments (Nov 2018)
- **Investment Endorsement:** All budgeted investments requiring Board approval (Equity, Corporate Restructuring, sustainable CAPEX/OPEX) must be endorsed by the GCFO prior to approval.
- **Consultancy & Professional Services:** 
    - Projects > RM30k / S$ 10k require pre-approval from the President, GCFO, or COO.
    - Engagements > 3 months and > RM30k / S$ 10k cumulatively require Group CEO approval.
- **Litigation & Disputes:** CEO approval is mandatory before commencing or settling any litigation claims or disputes.
- **Project Specific Limits:** NISS Aljunied Project operates under an elevated limit (Group CEO up to S$ 2M).

## Exception Governance (2026)
- **Financial Deviations:** Any deviations from the standard [[Financial Approval Matrix]] or fee policies (e.g., ad-hoc discounts or refunds) must be submitted via the 'Exception Request Form'. 
- **Operational Requirement:** These requests must be concise, action-oriented, and cite the specific policy clause being bypassed. This ensures that while the FAM provides the authority limits, the Exception process provides the formal record for 'Why' a limit was exceeded or a policy waived.
