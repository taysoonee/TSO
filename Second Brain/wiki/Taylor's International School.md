## Strategic Expansion Framework (Historical Baseline)
- **Gated Approach:** New projects must pass through a 3-gate qualification process. 
    - **Gate 1:** Focuses on geographical alignment (Malaysia), land size (min 6 acres), and financial viability (Max 15-year payback, 3-5 year breakeven).
- **Investment Models:**
    - **Model 1:** 100% TEG ownership of both Asset Co (Land/Building) and Op Co (School).
    - **Model 2:** JV structure with TEG holding min 30% in Asset Co and min 51% in Op Co.
    - **Model 3:** Pure lease model (Asset Co not owned by TEG) with 50-60 year lease terms.
- **Key Target Markets:** Klang Valley (multi-campus focus), Penang, Ipoh, JB, Kota Kinabalu, Kuching, Kuantan, Melaka, and Seremban.
- **Inter-Campus Spacing:** Multi-campuses within the same territory (e.g., Klang Valley) must maintain a minimum radius of >15km to prevent cannibalisation. [[Market Intelligence]]

## Operational Structure: The TIS Hub
- **Mechanism:** Administrative and non-academic functions for TIS campuses are centralised at the **TIS Hub**.
- **Leadership:** The Hub is led by the **TIS Chief Operating Officer (COO)**.
- **Scope:** This centralisation allows campuses to focus on academic delivery while the Hub handles support operations.

## Historical K-12 Expansion Blueprint: Project Asoka (Nov 2012)
- **Overview:** Project Asoka represents the structural and geographical framework for scaling the [[Taylor's International School]] brand across Malaysia.
- **Catchment Metric (Thesis 4 Validation):** Data confirmed 2/3 of parents stay less than 10km away. Therefore, 15km proximity is established as the absolute limit for catchment area, feeding directly into [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
- **Target Geographies:** Prioritized Klang Valley (multi-campus expansion, requiring >15km radius between campuses), Penang, JB, Kota Kinabalu, Kuching, and Ipoh. Secondary markets included Melaka and Seremban.
- **Campus Sizing & Capacity:** Defined scalable models ranging from a Small School (6 acres, 590 capacity) to Large-Sized 2 (10-15 acres, 2,500 capacity).
- **Investment Architecture:** 
    - **Model 1:** 100% TEG owned Asset and OpCo.
    - **Model 2:** JV Asset Co (TEG holding min 30%, 50-year lease) & OpCo (TEG holding min 51%).
    - **Model 3:** Lease model (Asset Co not TEG owned, OpCo 100% TEG, 50-60 year lease).
- **Financial Viability Hard Gates:** Required max payback period of 15 years (preference for <12 years) and operational breakeven within 3-5 years. This foundational metric guides all current and future brownfield/greenfield evaluations.
- **Source:** [[PMO Mtg 02B - Nov 2012 TIS S-BOX AMENDED %28F%29.pptx]]

## Update (May 13, 2026): TIS Portfolio Attrition & Catchment Data (2018-2021)
- **Catchment Disparity:** 95-100% of TISKL's new enrolment originates within a 20km radius, heavily validating [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]]. Conversely, TISPC exhibits a broader catchment, with 27-30% of enrolment drawn from beyond 20km.
- **Feeder Demographics:** Chinese vernacular schools represent the largest feeder demographic for TISPC and the second largest for TISKL. Kindergartens remain the primary feeder for TISKL. "Upgraders" from budget or learning centres account for ~30% of new intakes, while the capture of "downgraders" from premium tiers remains low. 
- **Expat Flight & Demographics:** Post-COVID data reveals a spike in expatriate attrition, with 1/3 of exiting expats returning to their home countries. The top flight-risk nationalities are China, India, and South Korea. This closely tracks the macroeconomic conditions outlined in [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Financial Hardship Thresholds:** For families citing "Financial Hardship" and downgrading to budget tier options (e.g., Kingsley, Maplewood), the tuition fee savings target ranges between RM10k and RM24k. This provides a quantifiable pressure point supporting [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].
- **Data Governance Gaps:** A critical blind spot in [[Admissions & Operations]] is identified, with ~34% (TISKL) and ~28% (TISPC) of previous school data missing for new enrolments, hindering precise competitor benchmarking.

## Attrition Analysis (2018-2021)
- **Expat Flight:** Post-COVID, expat attrition increased significantly, accounting for up to 66.7% of TISKL's attrition and 63.9% of TISPC's in 2021. The top 3 nationalities leaving were China, India, and South Korea, predominantly returning to their home countries. This directly links to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Domestic Squeeze:** Local attrition due to "Financial Hardship" spiked in 2020-2021 (reaching 13.4% at TISKL and 13.3% at TISPC). Students migrating for financial reasons typically left for budget schools with a fee difference of RM10k-RM24k, such as [[Tzu Chi International School]], Maplewood, and Collins. This corroborates [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].
- **Attrition Hotspots:** The highest attrition rates are concentrated in the Early Years Centre (EYC) (especially Nursery) and Primary levels.
- **Feeder Opportunities:** Historical new enrolments have shown strong intake from Chinese Vernacular Schools (historically up to 29% for TISPC) and other international schools like Sayfol, Tenby, and Rafflesia.

## Strategic History & Expansion Framework (Nov 2012)

In November 2012, Taylor's Education Group (TEG) established the 'ASOKA' framework for TIS expansion in Malaysia, focusing on a gated investment approach and specific land/capacity models.

### Investment Gating Protocol
- **Gate 1 (Qualify):** Assessed by President & Group CFO. Focus: Geography, Opportunity Size, Potential Impact, Shareholding %.
- **Gate 2 (Qualify):** Assessed by Group CFO, Division COO/CFO. Focus: 'Back of Envelope' Economics, Site Visit, Marketplace Assessment.
- **Gate 3 (Qualify):** Assessed by Finance, Legal, Marketing, HR, Academic. Focus: Full Due Diligence, 5-year Strategic Outlook, Operational Plan.

### Campus Size & Capacity Models
- **Small School:** 590 capacity | 6 acres (Benchmark: [[Australian International School Malaysia|AISM]])
- **Mid-Sized:** 1,030 - 1,660 capacity | 6 - 8 acres (Benchmark: [[Garden International School|SGI]] 6.4 acres)
- **Large-Sized:** 2,060 - 2,500 capacity | 8 - 15 acres (Benchmark: [[Taylor's International School Puchong|TISP]] 8 acres)

### Catchment & Location Criteria
- **The 10km Rule:** 2/3 of parents stay less than 10km away. Within 15km is considered the maximum ideal radius.
- **Multi-campus Buffer:** New campuses should be at least 15km apart to avoid cannibalisation.
- **Urban Density:** Target urban/main suburbs with proximity to major residential developments.

### Financial Benchmarks
- **Payback Period:** Maximum 15 years (Preference for < 12 years).
- **Breakeven:** Operational breakeven targeted within 3-5 years.

Source: [[PMO Mtg 02B - Nov 2012 TIS S-BOX AMENDED (F) - PDF.pdf]]
