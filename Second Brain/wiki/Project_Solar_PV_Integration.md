# Project: Solar PV Integration (2020-2023)

**Status:** Completed

## 1. Executive Summary

The Solar PV Integration project was a multi-year, portfolio-wide capital expenditure initiative to install solar photovoltaic systems across Taylor's Schools' key campuses. This programme serves as a primary operational hedge against utility inflation and energy price volatility.

By proactively managing and stabilising a significant operational cost, this project directly supports [[Thesis 2: The Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]] by reducing the pressure for OPEX-driven tuition fee increases. This enhances long-term financial stability and reinforces our competitive positioning within the price-sensitive domestic market. The project also provides a strong proof point for the group's commitment to [[ESG]] principles.

## 3. Strategic Rationale &amp; Documentation

The decision to proceed was driven by the successful pilot at Taylor's University, escalating material and energy costs, and the limited availability of the government's Net Energy Metering (NEM) 3.0 quota. The initiative leverages government incentives such as the Green Investment Tax Allowance (GITA) where applicable.

Structural assessments and technical planning were critical pre-requisites, ensuring roof load limits were verified before capital commitment.
