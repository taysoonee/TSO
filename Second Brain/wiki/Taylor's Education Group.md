## Employee Benefits (Historical Policy 2016)
Taylor's Education Group maintains a standardized Medical & Healthcare Plan for permanent and fixed-term employees in Malaysia.
- **Outpatient Medical:** Cashless facility at panel clinics for employees and dependents. Includes GP, Specialist, and child vaccinations (under 1 year). 
- **Annual Limits (Employee Only):** Dental, Annual Medical Check-ups, and Optical care are capped at a combined **RM 500** per annum, with Optical specifically capped at **RM 200**.
- **GHS (Hospitalization):** Coverage varies by job level. Top & Senior Management receive coverage for both employees and dependents. Middle Management & Others are covered for employees only (except for Taylor's College/University expats).
- **GPA (Group Personal Accident):** Sum insured equivalent to **48 months** basic salary.
- **Source:** [[Medical & Healthcare Plan 2016 - Policy.pdf]]

## Core EMO Strategic Pillars
Based on internal/competitor corporate strategy data (2023), education management organizations (EMOs) within the region structure their K-12 operations around five core capabilities to drive portfolio growth:
1. **Acquisition:** Driving inorganic growth through strategic M&A and land acquisition for greenfield developments. Links to [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
2. **Construction and upgrading works:** Maintaining competitive advantage via continuous CapEx and state-of-the-art infrastructure.
3. **Managing schools with different curricula:** Achieving operational flexibility by offering multiple curricula (UK, IB, Australian) to capture a broader market share across different price points. Links to [[Competitor Analysis]].
4. **Branding:** Differentiating the portfolio in a crowded market to drive top-of-funnel [[Admissions & Operations]] metrics.
5. **Nurturing learners:** Delivering academic excellence and pastoral care as the fundamental retention mechanism.

## Strategic Brand Expansions
In 2026, [[Taylor's Education Group]] launched **Lexel**, a new affordable international school brand in partnership with Gamuda Land and IDEO. This launch is targeted at capturing the 34% growth in the mid-market segment and provides a defensive "Shield Brand" to protect premium equity while adapting to macro pressures like the [[Taxation & Fiscal Policy]] SST implementation. This expansion is actively fueled by the minority stake from [[KKR (Kohlberg Kravis Roberts)]].
