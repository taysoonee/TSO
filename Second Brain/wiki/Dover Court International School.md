# Dover Court International School

# Overview
Dover Court International School (Singapore) is identified as a significant mid-to-premium tier competitor in the Singapore K-12 market. It is part of the Nord Anglia Education group.

# Competitive Positioning
- **Pricing:** Characterized as a lower-priced alternative to [[Nexus International School Singapore]] (NISS). 
- **Market Threat:** NISS parents have explicitly cited Dover Court's fee structure as a driver for potential withdrawal, particularly for self-funded families.
- **Inducements:** Reported to be active in offering "inducements" to capture market share in a "tough" Singaporean market (2026).

# Intelligence Updates
## May 14, 2026: NISS Price Pressure
- Internal reports from NISS indicate that Dover Court's pricing is a major competitive friction point.
- NISS admissions have used **Registration Fee waivers (SGD 2,000)** as a tactical counter-measure to retain families considering a move to Dover Court.
- This highlights the increasing elasticity of the "Premium" segment in Singapore to fee variations, validating [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Historical Baseline (2017-2018)
- **Mainstream Pricing:** Full-day Nursery started at S$19,590, scaling to S$30,000 for Sixth Form/IBDP (approx. RM 93,300 using the 2017 3.11 SGD/MYR exchange rate). This establishes the foundational pricing curve prior to recent fee pressures highlighted by [[Nexus International School Singapore]].
- **SEN Premium Strategy:** Dover Court operated a highly structured 'Department for Supportive Education' (DSE). DSE fees commanded a substantial premium (e.g., DSE IBDP at S$37,950 vs. Mainstream S$30,000, representing a ~26.5% markup). Auxiliary therapy sessions (Speech, Physio, Psychology) were billed granularly at S$160/hour. This validates the high-margin potential of specialised learning support within K-12.
- **Operations:** Maintained predictable cashflow via a mandatory S$1,500/year Building Fund Fee and strict termly withdrawal deadlines.

## Historical Baseline (2018-2019)
- **Mainstream Pricing:** Nursery (Full Day) at S$20,475 (~RM 69,615), scaling to S$31,350 (~RM 106,590) for Sixth Form/IBDP. This represents a measured year-on-year increase from 2017-2018, reinforcing its position as a highly competitive premium-tier alternative to [[Nexus International School Singapore]].
- **SEN Revenue Strategy (Pathways 3 & 4):** Continued aggressive monetization of its Supportive Education tracks. Secondary SEN tuition (Years 12-13) peaked at S$39,660 (~RM 134,844). Therapy fees (Speech, Physio, Occupational, Psychology) held firm at S$160/hour (~RM 544/hour). This consistent premium for specialized care validates SEN as a highly resilient and lucrative utility for the expatriate market, linking to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Ancillary Fees:** Imposed a non-refundable Registration Fee of S$2,800, an Application Fee of S$1,000, and maintained the termly Building Fund Fee (S$1,500/year). 

## Historical Baseline (2022-2023)
- **Mainstream Pricing:** Nursery (EYFS) at SGD 23,601, scaling to SGD 36,891 for Sixth Form/IBDP (Year 13).
- **SEN Revenue Strategy (Pathways 2 & 3):** Supportive Education tracks continue to command high premiums. Pathway 3 Early Intervention reached SGD 42,057, and Year 13 Pathway 3 peaked at SGD 46,548.
- **Ancillary Fees & Escalations:** Maintained the termly Building Fund Fee (SGD 505/term, total SGD 1,515/year). Application Fee increased to SGD 1,020, and the one-time Registration Fee increased to SGD 3,060, indicating continued strong demand and pricing power.

## May 14, 2026: 2023-2024 Fee Structure Update
- **Mainstream Pricing:** Nursery at S5,488, scaling to S9,843 for Year 13.
- **SEN Revenue Strategy:** Pathway 3 Early Intervention (Nursery & Reception) at S5,000, and Secondary Pathway 3 (Year 12-13) at S9,806.
- **Ancillary Fees:** Registration Fee remains S,060, Application Fee remains S,020. The Building Fund Fee is S,020 (total S,060 annually). Therapy fees for individual sessions increased to S00/hour (from S98).
- **Payment Structure:** Termly invoicing remains standard. Late fees of S00 per notice, with 2-5% interest on outstanding balances.
This pricing trend reinforces the competitive landscape, aligning with [[The Living Ledger]].
