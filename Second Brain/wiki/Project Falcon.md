# Project Falcon

**Project Falcon** refers to the March 2023 M&A evaluation of **St. John International School (SJIS)**. The primary objective was to assess SJIS as a potential entry point for Taylor's Schools into the budget international school segment.

## Strategic Fit & Evaluation
- **Brand Alignment:** The evaluation concluded that entering the budget segment via SJIS **does NOT fit** with Taylor's Schools' brand vision. TSO's purpose is to be a world-leading K-12 provider through high-quality, high-value education, rather than mass/budget education.
- **Brand Vulnerability:** The St. John brand was assessed as losing prestige and becoming increasingly vulnerable to higher-quality competitors in the budget/mid-tier space, such as [[Nobel International School]], [[Tzu Chi International School]], [[Sri Kuala Lumpur International School]], Sayfol, and Sri Emas.

## Financial Viability
- **Profitability & Margins:** The overall PBT margin was 25%, with an EBITDA margin of 27%, sustained only by low CAPEX and minimal OPEX typical of budget operations.
- **Rental Burden:** Total rental costs were unsustainably high at 16% of revenue. 
    - **Primary School:** Rental was 21% of revenue. The segment was loss-making (-19% PBT margin).
    - **Secondary School:** Rental was 15% of revenue, operating at a +37% PBT margin.
- **Utilisation:** 
    - Primary: 50% capacity (361/660).
    - Secondary: 75% capacity (807/1,075).
- **Macro Market Context:** At the time of evaluation, the budget segment in the Klang Valley showed a 5% CAGR in enrolment (2017–2020), but fee growth was stagnant at 2%.

## Academic & Operational Red Flags
- **Academic Performance:** A notable decline in academic results was observed. IGCSE A/A* rates dropped from 53% in 2021 to just 37% in 2022.
- **Teacher Retention:** The staff turnover rate was high, averaging ~20%.
- **Facilities & HSE Risks:**
    - **Primary Campus:** Located within the St. John government school building. The space was restricted (locked gates) with very limited, shared sports facilities. The demographic split was 30% Indian expats, with the rest being local Malaysians.
    - **Secondary Campus:** Occupied 9 out of 16 floors in the commercial Ming Building, sharing 4 lifts with commercial offices (only 2 reserved for the school). This presented significant Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE) issues, including cramped areas and lack of dedicated outdoor Co-Curricular Activity (CCA) fields (which had to be rented). Demographics included 20-25% Asian expats.

## Market Cannibalisation Risk
- A critical risk identified was the potential **cannibalisation of the Mid-Priced Market** from Taylor's own [[Taylor's International School]] (specifically TISKL). 
- If price-sensitive parents within the 10km radius associated the new budget school with Taylor's quality (minus the premium facilities), it could draw enrolment away from TISKL, eroding overall portfolio margins (linking back to [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]]).

## Update (May 13, 2026): M&A Feasibility (Project Falcon v2.8)
- **Strategic Fit:** Confirmed rejection of St. John's International School for budget segment entry. TSO's vision remains high-quality, high-value education, which SJIS fails to meet due to low academic quality and brand degradation.
- **Financial Details:** Validated unsustainably high rental overheads (16% of revenue). Primary segment is operating at a loss (-19% PBT) with only 50% utilisation, while the Secondary segment operates at +37% PBT with 75% utilisation. Overall EBITDA margin is 27%, propped up by minimal OPEX/CAPEX.
- **Operational HSE Risks:** Secondary school operations in a commercial building (Ming Building, sharing 4 lifts across 9 floors) present critical HSE risks. Primary campus relies on leased space from a government school.
- **Market Impact:** Acquiring a budget school within a 10km radius poses a severe cannibalization risk to TISKL's mid-priced market, as price-sensitive parents may downgrade, validating [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Update (May 14, 2026): M&A Feasibility Review (Project Falcon)
- **Strategic Confirmation:** Review of the primary slide deck reinforces the rejection of the SJIS acquisition for entering the budget segment. The systemic issues of declining academic quality (IGCSE A/A* dropped to 37% in 2022), high rental overheads (16% of revenue), and brand degradation against competitors like [[Nobel International School]], [[Tzu Chi International School]], and [[Sri Kuala Lumpur International School]] are critical deterrents.
- **Demographics & Utilisation:** Primary segment (50% capacity) has 30% Indian expats, operating at a loss. Secondary segment (75% capacity) has 20-25% Asian expats. High structural risks exist due to shared commercial facilities.
- **Market Dynamics:** Budget segment historic CAGR was 5% (2017-2020) with a 2% fee growth, highlighting price sensitivity. Acquiring this asset risks cannibalising TISKL’s mid-priced demographic, aligning with the pressures seen in [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].
