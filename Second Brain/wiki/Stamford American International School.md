# Stamford American International School (SAIS)

## Overview
Stamford American International School (SAIS) is a premium international school located in Singapore, operated by the Cognita group. 

## Pricing Shift: 2017-2018 Historical Baseline
This baseline provides context for Singapore's premium education costs, highlighting the cost-arbitrage opportunity for Malaysian schools targeting regional expats ([[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]).
*   **Annual Tuition Fees:** Ranged from SGD 14,954 (MYR ~46,200) for 3-day Pre-Nursery to SGD 40,206 (MYR ~124,236) for Grade 9-12.
*   **Non-Refundable Entry Fees:** Application Fee of SGD 3,160 (MYR ~9,764) plus a Facility Fee of SGD 2,675 (MYR ~8,265) for Early Learning and SGD 7,490 (MYR ~23,144) for Woodleigh Campus.
*   **Mandatory Add-ons:** Annual Technology Fee of SGD 850 - 1,070 (MYR ~2,626 - 3,306). Academic Field Studies required for Grade 3-12, costing up to SGD 3,324 (MYR ~10,271) per trip.
*   **Sibling Discounts:** 50% off application/facility fees for 3rd child. Tuition discounts: 10% (3rd child), 20% (4th child).

## Strategic Implication for Taylor's Schools
The high absolute cost of premium schooling in Singapore (even at 2017 levels) validates the regional cost-competitiveness of Taylor's Schools. It supports the thesis that cross-border expat relocation from Singapore to Malaysia (e.g., Johor or KL) is driven by significant educational savings. Linked to [[Competitor Analysis]].

## Pricing Shift: 2022-2023 Early Learning Village (EYC) Update
SAIS's 2022-2023 fee structure reinforces their position at the extreme premium end of the Singaporean market, creating a strong contrast that supports [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] by highlighting Malaysia's cost-efficiency for expatriates.
* **Infant Care (2-18 months):** 5-day programs run at SGD 2,832/month (MYR ~9,912). 
* **Early Years Annual Tuition:** 5-Day Full programs range from SGD 26,724 (MYR ~93,534) for Nursery to SGD 41,093 (MYR ~143,825) for KG2.
* **Aggressive Early-Capture Strategy:** SAIS waives the substantial mainstream enrolment fee of SGD 3,493 (MYR ~12,225) for families who enroll in Infant Care for at least 6 months and transition to mainstream Early Years. This locks in the primary funnel at an extremely early stage (2-18 months), preventing competitor poaching. This strategy is a highly localized application of hyper-local demographic capture, mirroring the competitive intensity seen in [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
* **EAL Premium:** Mandatory English as an Additional Language fees add SGD 7,587 (MYR ~26,554) to SGD 12,406 (MYR ~43,421) annually, heavily taxing non-native speaking expatriates.
* **Logistics & Cross-Enrolment:** Free dedicated transportation is provided for Early Learning Village (ELV) students living around or with siblings at the main Woodleigh Campus, explicitly removing logistical friction for multi-child families.

## Update (May 14, 2026): 2015 Financial & Growth Baseline
- **Hyper-Growth Phase:** In 2015, SAIS demonstrated massive market capture, posting a 39.8% YoY revenue growth to reach SGD 78.8M (~MYR 274.2M). 
- **Margin Compression:** Despite the massive top-line growth, PBT was highly compressed at SGD 1.0M (Margin ~1.3%), validating the heavy operational and capital costs required to aggressively acquire expatriate market share. This aligns with the dynamics of [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]].
