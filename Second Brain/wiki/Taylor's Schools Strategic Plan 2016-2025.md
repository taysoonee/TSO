# Taylor's Schools Strategic Plan 2016-2025

This document outlines the 2016-2025 strategic plan for Taylor's Schools, as presented in October 2016.

## Mission 2016-2025
- **To lay the foundation of lifelong success for our learners.**
- **To be a premier global K-12 education provider.**

## Executive Summary

- **Growth (2006-2015):** From 1 to 7 schools, 178% enrolment growth (3,317 to 9,214), and 824% revenue growth (RM63.83m to RM589.74m).
- **Key Challenges:**
    - Increasingly competitive market with a 124% increase in international schools since 2010.
    - New competitors from international brands (e.g., GEMS, Nord Anglia) and developer-backed schools.
    - Internal bureaucracy affecting agile decision-making.
    - Lack of clarity on long-term Group financial plans.
- **Core Strategies:**
    1.  **Strengthen Customer Value Proposition (CVP):** Improve facilities, enhance branding, and offer scholarship programs.
    2.  **Talent Attraction & Retention:** Implement staff performance management and professional development.
    3.  **Cost Reduction:** Staff localization program.
- **Projected Impact (2015-2020):**
    - **Enrolment:** +39% (from 6,688 to 9,269)
    - **Revenue:** +78% (from RM331.4m to RM591.1m)
    - **PBT:** +99% (from RM43.3m to RM86.2m)
- **International Expansion:** Expansion of the [[Nexus International School]] brand to Singapore.

## Market Analysis
- **Market Growth:** 205% growth in the number of international schools since the 2010 liberalization.
- **Projected Unfilled Capacity (2020):**
    - **Premium Market (>RM40,000):** 44%
    - **Mid-price Market (RM20,000-RM40,000):** 58%
- **Competition:** Stiff competition from new entrants in the Klang Valley.

## School-Specific Strategies

- **[[Garden International School]]**: Consolidation as a premier "premium international school".
- **[[Taylor's International School Puchong]]**: Consolidation as a premier “mid-priced international school”.
- **[[Taylor's International School Kuala Lumpur]]**: Aggressive growth as a premier “mid-priced international school”.
- **[[Australian International School Malaysia]]**: Conservative growth as a “premium international school” with an Australian curriculum.
- **[[Nexus International School Malaysia]]**: Steady growth, strengthening its position as a value “premium international school”.
- **[[Nexus International School Singapore]]**: Conservative growth as a “premium international school” with an IB curriculum.
- **[[International School Kuantan]]**: Decision on TIS vs. GIS branding vs. divestment.

## CAPEX for Committed Projects (Total: RM837.8 million)

- **[[Nexus International School Singapore]] (New Campus at Aljunied):** RM734 million
- **[[Garden International School]] (Elevated Car park, Additional Learning Spaces, Auditorium, Land Premium):** RM26 million
- **[[Taylor's International School Puchong]] (Phase 2, 6th floor classrooms + common area):** RM27.3 million
- **[[Taylor's International School Kuala Lumpur]] (Swimming Pool, Changing Rooms, Performing Arts Facilities):** RM11.7 million
- **[[Australian International School Malaysia]] (Car park):** RM2.8 million
- **[[International School Kuantan]] (New Campus, Land Premium, Divest):** RM36 million
