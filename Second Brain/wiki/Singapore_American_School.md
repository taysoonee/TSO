# Singapore American School (SAS)

Singapore American School (SAS) is a major Tier-1 competitor in the Singapore premium international education market. It operates as a non-profit, company limited by guarantee with charitable status. It is one of the largest American-sponsored overseas schools.

## Strategic Positioning
SAS positions itself as a world leader in education, offering an American educational experience with an international perspective. Its strategy focuses on cultivating exceptional thinkers through defined 21st-century outcomes and institutional commitments to professional learning, curriculum quality, and technology integration.
[ARCHIVE: SAS - Wasc report.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2013]

The school targets the expatriate market, with a specific focus on US citizens. This is evident in its differentiated registration fee structure. This positioning makes it highly sensitive to shifts in the [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate &amp; FDI Inflow Vector]].

## Financial Performance

### FY2017 (Year ended 30 June 2017)
- **Revenue:** S\$153,585,000
- **Profit (Surplus):** S\$21,130,000
- **Student Count (Members):** 4,075 (a slight decrease from 4,322 in FY2016)
- **Strategic Note:** The school's non-profit model focuses on reinvesting surpluses into its educational provision and long-term capital expenditures, such as the Woodlands Campus replacement. This demonstrates robust long-term financial planning and stability.

### FY2014 (Data from WASC Report)
- **Operating Budget:** USD 134,000,000
- **Student Count:** 3,935 (69% US Citizens)
- **Strategic Note:** In response to increasing competition and rising energy costs, the school benchmarked a 3.5% annual tuition increase to reduce its premium over other Singapore schools and invested in solar panels, an early indicator of the [[ESG|Solar PV Integration]] strategy later adopted by Taylor's Schools. It also secured S\$60M in credit lines as a backstop against potential enrolment decline.

## Fee Structure Analysis
SAS maintains a premium fee structure with significant one-time registration and annual facility fees. This demonstrates a strategy to secure high upfront commitment from families.

- **2023-2024:** Total annual fees for new, non-US students in High School were projected at S\$59,350 (2023) and S\$59,800 (2024). The school factored in Singapore's GST increase from 8% to 9%.
[ARCHIVE: SAS 2023.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2023-2024]

- **2022-2023:** Total annual fees for new, non-US High School students were S\$57,075. The school differentiates registration fees based on US passport/Green Card status (S\$7,490 vs S\$9,630).
[ARCHIVE: SAS 2022.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2022-23]

- **2021-2022:** Total annual fees for a new, non-US High School student reached S\$55,724.
[ARCHIVE: SAS 2021-22.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2021-2022]

- **2017-2018:** Registration fees were S\$7,490 for US passport holders vs S\$9,630 for others.
[ARCHIVE: SAS Fee 2017.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2017-18]

- **2016-2017:** A new student registration fee of S\$12,840 was in place, alongside an annual facility fee of S\$5,029.
[ARCHIVE: SAS Fee 2016.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2016]

- **2015-2016:** A new student registration fee of S\$12,840 was charged.
[ARCHIVE: SAS Fee 2015.pdf -&gt; 78. Financial &amp; Audited Reports/2 Singapore Schools/Singapore American School/2015]

- **2014-2015:** A new student registration fee of S\$12,840 was charged.
