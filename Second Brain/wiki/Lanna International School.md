# Lanna International School (LANNA)

## Overview
Lanna International School, located in Chiang Mai, Thailand, provides critical insight into the Northern Thailand premium education market. As Taylor's Schools monitors regional growth corridors across ASEAN (validating [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]), LANNA serves as a case study in post-Covid demographic shifts and strategic infrastructure investments.

## Demographic & Market Shifts
Post-Covid, the demographic makeup of the school shifted dramatically. 
- **The Chinese Influx:** Chinese student enrolment exploded from roughly 5% pre-Covid to 44%, placing them on par with Thai domestic students. 
- **ELL Pressures:** This massive influx radically altered the English Language Learner (ELL) landscape, requiring substantial operational adaptations within [[Admissions & Operations]] and academic departments to maintain high IGCSE and A-Level standards.

## Pricing Dynamics
Tuition fees at LANNA and in Northern Thailand generally average THB 300,000 (approx. RM 36,300). This is 35-40% cheaper than the Bangkok market, where fees range from THB 700,000 to 900,000 (approx. RM 84,700 - RM 108,900). This affordability makes secondary cities highly attractive to expats and aligns with regional constraints seen in [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Infrastructure & ESG Leadership
LANNA has undergone significant physical expansion, including new G1/G2 Buildings, Science Labs, and an Innovation Building. 
Crucially, their infrastructure upgrades included major community value-adds:
- **Clean Water Pipeline:** To secure clean city water for the campus, LANNA invested over THB 3.2 million (approx. RM 387,200) to lay a 3km pipeline. This pipeline was made accessible to all "moo baans" (villages) in the San Phak Wan Sub District, which previously relied on underground bore water. This is a remarkable demonstration of [[ESG]] integration.
- **Electrical Extension:** An investment of THB 3.15 million (approx. RM 381,150) was made for main electricity extension and underground wiring.

## Environmental Risks
The school faces acute challenges during Chiang Mai's "Smoke Season" (where AQI frequently exceeds 200). This environmental hazard causes some young families to relocate and necessitates significant capital expenditure on indoor air purification systems, representing a growing climate-related [[Risk Management]] factor for regional expansion.
