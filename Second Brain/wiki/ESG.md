# ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)

ESG initiatives within [[Taylor's Schools]] are a strategic focus area, tying into long-term operational sustainability and cost optimisation, as well as mitigating future risks (see [[The Living Ledger]] and [[Risk Management]]).

## 2023 Solar System Rollout
A major initiative in 2023 involved the deployment of solar power systems across four core K-12 campuses: [[Garden International School]] (GIS), [[Australian International School Malaysia]] (AISM), [[Taylor's International School Kuala Lumpur|Taylor's International School KL]] (TISKL), and TIS Puchong (TISPC). 

As of January 2023, while site surveys and energy logging were completed for most campuses, the project faced administrative bottlenecks related to TNB Change of Tenancy requirements, which threatened the timeline for subsequent SEDE NEM and ST Public License approvals.

- **Source:** [[Progress Update.pptx]]

## 2020 Taylor's University Solar Precedent
In September 2020, [[Taylor's University]] received an EPCC proposal from Plus Solar Systems Sdn Bhd for a 779.24 kWp Photovoltaic System. The project carried a CAPEX of RM 1,839,000.00 but promised an estimated Net Saving of RM 8,765,343.18 over 25 years with a highly attractive Payback Period of 3.03 Years. This high-ROI university-level initiative likely served as the foundational blueprint and financial justification for the broader K-12 campus solar rollout that followed in 2023.

## International School of Beijing (ISB) Sustainability Case Study
As presented at ACAMIS (April 2021), the International School of Beijing (ISB) established a "Sustainability Roadmap 2025," providing a robust framework for ESG integration in K-12 education. This aligns with [[Taylor's Schools]]' own sustainability and cost-optimisation strategies.

- **Financial Impact:** ISB reported a 26% year-on-year reduction in energy and water spending in 2020, yielding cost savings of 2.6 million RMB (approx. 1.69 million MYR). This demonstrates the tangible ROI of ESG initiatives, similar to the [[Taylor's University]] solar precedent.
- **Operational Tactics:** Key initiatives included deploying 27 electric buses (representing 50% of their fleet), upgrading to LED lighting, and eliminating single-use plastics in the cafeteria through a partnership with Sodexo.
- **Biophilia Design:** ISB incorporated biophilia design (e.g., wood paneling, natural color highlights) to enhance occupant connectivity to nature, which is linked to improved student and staff wellbeing and performance.
- **Brand Differentiator:** ISB publishes an annual 'Sustainability Impact Report', using ESG as a core marketing pillar. This approach provides a blueprint for how Taylor's could position its own ESG initiatives to enhance brand value. 
- **Global Benchmarking:** ISB highlighted [[American School of Dubai]], [[Dulwich College]], and [[Munich International School]] as peers with leading sustainability programs. (See [[Competitor Analysis]])
- **Source:** [[ACAMIS+Sustainable+Schools+Presentation.pdf]]

Missing this threatened to cascade delays into the TNB NEM Assessment and SEDE NEM Application phases, pushing back projected operational cost savings and [[ESG]] milestones. This highlights the friction in cross-entity real estate management within [[Taylor's Education Group]] when executing portfolio-wide CAPEX initiatives.
