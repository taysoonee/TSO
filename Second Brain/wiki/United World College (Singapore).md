# United World College (UWC) Singapore

## Overview
United World College of South East Asia (UWCSEA) is an ultra-premium, high-demand international school in Singapore.

## Update (May 14, 2026): 2018-2019 Fee Hike
- **Pricing Shift:** Internal tracking confirms UWC implemented a +9% fee increase for the 2018-2019 academic cycle. This aggressive inflation mirrors moves by other legacy top-tier schools (TTS +10%, SAIS +13%), pushing the absolute price ceiling in Singapore higher.
- **Strategic Impact:** The consistent inflation at schools like UWC validates the high cost of living and education in Singapore, supporting [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] by highlighting the potential for regional expat relocation to more cost-effective hubs in Malaysia.
- **Source:** [[NISS v2.pptx]]


## 2014/2015 Baseline Fees & Structure
- **Tuition:** K1-G1 at SGD 9,095/term (~RM 84,310/year); G11-G12 at SGD 11,248/term (~RM 104,268/year).
- **Boarding:** SGD 10,492/term (~RM 97,260/year) starting from Grade 8. Combined Senior tuition and boarding totals ~RM 201,529/year.
- **Hidden Fees:** Development levy of SGD 2,290/term (Year 1) and compulsory trips ranging from SGD 450 to SGD 4,200.
- **Strategic Impact:** Establishing this high early baseline validates the immense price ceiling in the Singapore market. The significant capital requirements (e.g., SGD 3,210 enrolment fee) and additional fees support [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]], setting the stage for Taylor's Schools to capture regional expatriate overflow by offering high-quality, value-driven alternatives.
- **Source:** [[UWC - 2014.pdf]]

## Pricing Shift: 2019/2020 Fee Structure Update
- **Pricing Shift:** First-year school fees for G11-G12 reached SGD 47,085 (~MYR 164,800), and G1-G5 reached SGD 40,065 (~MYR 140,200). The non-refundable enrolment fee was increased to SGD 4,900 (~MYR 17,150).
- **Boarding:** Residential boarding fees for G8-G12 increased to SGD 36,700 (~MYR 128,450) annually, plus a SGD 300 amenities fund. Combined with senior tuition, a boarding student's cost approached ~MYR 293,000 per year.
- **Strategic Impact:** The prohibitive cost of top-tier Singaporean schools like UWCSEA (approaching MYR 300k/year for boarders) creates a significant opportunity for Taylor's Schools. It strongly supports [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]] by positioning Malaysian premium boarding (e.g., at Nexus) as a high-value, cost-effective alternative for regional ASEAN wealth and expatriates priced out of the Singapore market.
- **Source:** [[UWC - 2019.pdf]]

## Pricing Shift: 2024/2025 Fee Structure Update
- **Pricing Shift:** First-year school fees for G11-G12 reached SGD 56,361 (~MYR 174,155 at 3.09 MYR/SGD), and K1-G1 reached SGD 45,615 (~MYR 140,950). The enrolment fee increased to SGD 4,992 (~MYR 15,425), and now features an aggressive family acquisition discount (25% off for 3rd child, 50% off for 4th+).
- **Boarding:** Residential boarding fees for G8-G12 increased to SGD 41,472 (~MYR 128,148) annually, plus a SGD 317 amenities fund. Total Year 1 cost for a senior boarder (G11-G12) reaches SGD 98,150 (~MYR 303,283).
- **Strategic Impact:** The super-premium price ceiling continues to climb. Breaching the MYR 300k/year barrier for senior boarders presents a massive arbitrage opportunity for Taylor's premium Malaysian schools. This strongly validates [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]], positioning campuses like Nexus as high-value regional alternatives for FDI expats and ASEAN wealth.
- **Source:** [[UWC - 2024.pdf]]

## Pricing Shift (2018/19 Fee Hike)
For the 2018/19 academic year, [[United World College (Singapore)]] increased its fees by +9%, bringing the top-end (G11/12) fee to SGD 42,420 (~RM 148,470). This aligned with broad premium sector hikes across Singapore, validating strong demand and low price elasticity among elite expat enrollments connected to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
