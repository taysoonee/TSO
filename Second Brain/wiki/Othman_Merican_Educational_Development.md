# Othman Merican Educational Development Berhad

## Corporate Overview
Othman Merican Educational Development Berhad operates under a limited-by-guarantee structure as a private primary and secondary school operating in Subang Jaya. The entity wields a distinct competitive advantage over fully commercialised competitors via its tax-exempt, non-profit classification (Income Tax Exemption No. 5 Order 2008). 

## Financial Performance & Cash Positioning
Consolidated financial data spanning 2011 to 2015 establishes a resilient pattern of top-line growth. Revenue escalated from RM 30.3 million (2011) to RM 38.0 million (2015), consistently lifting net profits from RM 5.3 million to RM 7.0 million annually. Crucially, the institutional framework stockpiles massive cash assets; by Q4 2015, the school registered RM 39.3 million exclusively in fixed deposits. This potent capital baseline authorises sudden facility expansions or aggressive fee-suppression tactics, serving as a primary risk indicator for [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. Subang-centric Taylor's properties face a highly liquid competitor here.

## Operational Strategy
Historically, the entity prefers high-intensity capital reinvestment directly into physical learning spaces, evidenced by extensive 'construction in progress' ledgers (RM 8.16 million logged in 2015). They maintain a stable pedagogical employment base averaging 263 staff members, proving strong operational consistency that threatens our established domestic market shares tracked under [[Competitor Analysis]].
