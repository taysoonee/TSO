# BDRC International Schools Brand Equity & Market Insights Study (Singapore) 2023

## Executive Summary
This BDRC report (published 31 October 2023 for Nexus) assesses the brand equity and market insights of international schools in Singapore. The study identifies a rejuvenated inbound market of expats post-pandemic, but also increased pressure on expat incomes due to cost of living (rent) and economic uncertainty. There is a marked shift toward Asian expats, while Western expats are increasingly found in mixed marriages.

## Key Highlights & Trade-offs
- **Parent Demographics:** The leading nationalities for inbound expats are from India, UK, Singapore (returning/local), Japan, and Australia. 54% of expats are inbound from within Asia.
- **Payment of Fees:** Only 25% of parents have school fees paid for by their employers (often only partially). This benefit is far more common for Western expats (36%) compared to Asian expats (20%).
- **School Choice Drivers:**
    - **Academic Excellence** and **Life Skills** are the top priorities. However, when forced to make a trade-off, parents expect schools to invest most in Academic Excellence, particularly for Secondary years.
    - Demand for **Sports facilities** is rising and generally outweighs the demand for arts facilities.
    - **Parent Service** is highly valued, particularly in the lower secondary years.
- **Brand Equity Drivers:** The highest drivers of recommendation are:
    1. Good culture & social values
    2. Well-kept building, grounds, and facilities
    3. Reputation for academic excellence
    4. Innovative approach to learning
    5. Good for sports (climbed significantly in 2023)

## Net Promoter Score (NPS) Leaders
Based on those familiar with the schools:
- **UWC South East Asia:** +9
- **Knightsbridge House:** +6
- **Tanglin Trust School:** -5
- **XCL American Academy:** -5
- **XCL World Academy:** -7
- **Singapore American:** -10
*Note: The Singapore market generally exhibits negative NPS scores across the board, with UWC and Knightsbridge being rare exceptions.*

## Boarding Schools
About 25% of parents will consider boarding schools, almost entirely for Upper Secondary (Years 12-13). The leading destinations are the UK, USA, Australia, Malaysia, and Europe. Notably, 22% of those considering boarding would consider Malaysia (a slight increase), and 20% specifically Johore.

## Advertising & Recruitment
- **Word of Mouth** (65%) and **Internet Search** (40%) are the primary sources for discovering the chosen school.
- **Advertising** recall is generally high (76% recall seeing ads), with Digital and Social Media being the most recalled.
- **Nexus** has the 3rd highest advertising recall (22%) and is highly visible via outdoor advertising.
