# Taylor's Schools Financial Validation (Capstone)

## Nexus International School Singapore (NISS)
NISS continues to command the K-12 offshore portfolio. FY2023 outcomes confirmed SGD 75.5 million in revenue yielding a net annual profit of SGD 12.2 million. Handling a resilient capacity of 1,274 students, NISS demonstrates incredible elite market capture. Given regional SGD/MYR dynamics, the Aljunied asset acts as our primary group-level volatility hedge, perfectly executing the corporate mandates embedded within [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].

## Taylor's International School Kuala Lumpur (TISKL)
For the 2022/2023 cycle, the KL domestic campus validated strong domestic penetration, driving RM 78.5 million in revenues to net an RM 4.2 million margin. Supporting 1,842 students placed cleanly within the RM 32k-48k fee bracket, TISKL confirms high operational loyalty from the upper-M40/T20 segment despite regional subsidy threats indicated in [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. Most critically, structural solar capital investments (RM 493.9k) fully absorbed the expected inflationary shocks on utility budgets, defending our operational margins.
