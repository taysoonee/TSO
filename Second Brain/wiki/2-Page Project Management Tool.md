# 2-Page Project Management Tool

The 2-Page Project Management Tool is a concise framework used by Taylor's Education Group to plan, execute, and monitor projects efficiently. It focuses on clarity of scope, stakeholder buy-in, and measurable results.

## Core Methodology
The tool is structured around four critical questions/sections:

### A) Problem & Project Statement
- **Problem Statement:** Identifies the specific "gap" between the current state and the goal. It often includes metrics (e.g., "We have a gap of [X] which is impacting our [KPI] and this has been going on for the last [Y] months").
- **Project Statement:** A verb-led action statement (e.g., "To implement...", "To change...").

### B) Results
- Defined measurements of success.
- Must be specific, measurable, and time-bound (e.g., aligned with [[Market Intelligence]] KPIs).

### C) Approach
- **Barriers:** Identification of potential obstacles to project success.
- **Approaches:** Specific strategies or actions taken to remove or mitigate those barriers.

### D) Support Required (4W1H)
- Clearly identifies **Who, What, Why, How, & When**.
- Designed to obtain specific commitments from stakeholders (e.g., "I need Tommy's expertise on problem solving... I will need 50% of his time for the next 30 days").

## Implementation Tools
- **Gantt Chart:** Used to preview and negotiate deadlines with stakeholders.
- **Stakeholder Management Tool:** Integral to ensuring project buy-in.
- **Learning Journal 2.0:** A reflection component focusing on behavior change and sustained results.

## Strategic Intent & Action Learning (Update May 12, 2026)
- **Base-lining Efficiency:** The protocol aims to clarify expectations with key stakeholders in the shortest period with minimal rework.
- **Resource Prioritization:** The 'Problem Statement' is explicitly used to prioritize focus and resources, moving from symptoms to root cause 'Gaps'.
- **Proactive Barrier Management:** Strategies are developed *before* project start to manage barriers, reducing frustration and back-tracking in schedules.
- **Self-Monitoring:** Implementation of Gantt charts enables teams to move to self-monitoring performance feedback, ensuring 100% meeting of dead lines.
- **Documentation for Buy-in:** The 2-page document serves as the primary tool for securing stakeholder buy-in and support.
