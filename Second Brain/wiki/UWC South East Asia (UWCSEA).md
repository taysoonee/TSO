# UWC South East Asia (UWCSEA)

## Strategic Overview (2011-2012 Baseline)
UWCSEA operates on a "One College, Two Campuses" strategy in Singapore, acting as a major competitor in the regional premium education market. The 2011-2012 financial period marked significant expansion with the opening of the East Campus and curriculum standardisation via the Curriculum Articulation Project (K1 to Grade 12). 

Relevant connections: [[Singapore School Development Framework]], [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].

## Enrolment & Financials
- **Student Population:** Scaled to approximately 5,000 students across the Dover and East campuses.
- **Revenue:** Combined campus revenues exceeded S$123 million (Dover: S$82.1m; East: S$41.1m).
- **Scholarships:** Dedicated 3% of tuition fees to a scholarship programme (funded at S$4.21 million for 69 scholars), signalling a deliberate strategy to reinforce brand prestige and diversity without compromising their premium fee positioning.

## Operational Expansions
### Boarding & Pastoral Care
The introduction of Tampines House (East Campus) and the implementation of vertical boarding on the Dover campus underscores the regional demand for premium pastoral care infrastructure. This directly validates [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]], indicating that leading regional competitors view boarding as central to their value proposition for expatriate and wealthy ASEAN demographics.

### Infrastructure & ESG
UWCSEA's early commitment to environmental sustainability—including the Greenmark Platinum rating for the East Campus, comprehensive solar panel installations, and the Eco Village—established a high operational benchmark. This early adoption mirrors and validates Taylor's Schools' defensive strategy via the current portfolio-wide [[ESG|Solar PV Integration]] project, which mitigates constraints outlined in [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. Furthermore, capital investment in outdoor education at Twin Beach, Sibu, substantially extended their experiential learning footprint.
