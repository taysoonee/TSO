# Investment Committee (EIC)

The **Educrest Investment Committee (EIC)** is the primary body responsible for the assessment of all investments in building premises and land for the **Educrest** group of companies [[Investment Committee TOR 01.11.2013.pdf]].

## Formation & Objectives
Formed via circular resolution on 1 November 2013, the EIC's specific objectives include:
- Providing independent and market-relevant views on planned investments.
- Conducting qualitative and quantitative (technical and financial) assessments.
- Guiding the management team on setting and achieving metrics.
- Developing industry networking and relationships.

## Composition
The committee is composed of board members from **TEG Assets Sdn. Bhd.**. 
- **First EIC Members (2013):**
    - **Dato' Loy Teik Ngan:** EIC Chairman.
    - **Abby Lew Tong:** Committee Member.
- **Secretary:** The Company Secretary of TEG Assets Sdn. Bhd. serves as the EIC Secretary.

## The Gate Process
The EIC utilizes a "Gate" system to manage the lifecycle of project proposals:
- **Gate 1: Opportunity Identification:** Initial assessment of a potential investment opportunity.
- **Gate 2: Approach Endorsement:** Feasibility study and business case evaluation. Proposals at this stage include detailed financial modeling, risk assessments, and site visits.
- **Gate 3: Execution/Binding Offer:** Due diligence completion and final approval to execute the project or submit a binding offer.

## TEG Standard Information Pack (2026 Baseline)
The **Information Pack** is the mandatory governance document for any project or proposal requiring Board, Council, or GMC approval. It ensures that decisions are based on standardized financial and strategic criteria.

### Core Financial Metrics
- **NPV (Net Present Value):** Negative NPV projects require explicit justification.
- **IRR (Internal Rate of Return):** Standard measure for project viability.
- **ROI Lifecycle:** Calculations must cover the full life span of the project.
- **Payback Period:** Expressed in years.

### Governance & Endorsement Chain
1. **Preparation:** Finance Manager / Financial Controller.
2. **Review:** Principals / SVP / VP.
3. **Endorsement:** President / COO.
4. **Group Finality:** GCFO (and Group HR if applicable).

See also: [[Financial Approval Matrix]] and [[Taylor's Schools]] strategic governance.


### Standard Template Structure (Board Resolution Info Pack)
The standard template for a Board Resolution Information Pack requires the following sections:
- **Background:** Context and history of the issue.
- **Proposal:** The specific action requested.
- **Rationale:** The business case and strategic justification.
- **Effective Date:** When the proposal takes effect.
- **Actions to be Taken:** Implementation steps.
- **Financial Implication / Impact:** Detailed costs and budget impact.
- **Impact (Positive / Negative):** SWOT-style analysis of the decision.
- **Question & Answer:** Anticipated board queries.
- **Person to Contact:** Lead project owner.

*All content must contain clear measurable objectives, delivery deadlines, milestones, total costs, and a cost timetable.*

## Taylor's Management Investment Committee (TMIC)
Formed by the Board of Directors of **Educrest Sdn. Bhd.** on 5 November 2018, the **TMIC** evaluates investments ensuring acceptable financial returns. This authority was delegated to the Group CEO.

### TMIC Composition
The committee consists of at least three members, with the initial 2018 office bearers being:
- **President of TEPL:** Karl Edward Engkvist
- **Group CFO:** Katja Hildegard Dommers-Behrend
- **Vice President (Finance), TEPL:** Rosalind Yeoh Sun Looi
- **Senior Manager, Group Finance:** Koek Gim Aik

### Investment Scope and Authority
- All equity investments and other forms into public/private markets.
- Corporate structuring and restructuring exercises.
- All capital expenditure above **RM10 million** (including sustainable CapEx and OpEx).
*Reference: [[Financial Approval Matrix]]*

### Evaluation Parameters
The TMIC utilizes an evaluation approach focused on eight key areas (evaluated simultaneously or step-by-step):
1. **Strategy and Purpose:** Strategic intent, objectives, and rationale.
2. **Business Model:** Commercial and financial sustainability.
3. **SWOT Analysis:** Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and competitive landscape.
4. **Risks and Mitigants:** Financial, legal, regulatory, tax, reputation, and commercial risks.
5. **Financial Projections and Returns:** Base/Worst case scenarios, IRR, Cash-on-cash multiple, Payback, NPV, Sources/Uses of Funds.
6. **Management:** Key team leading the investment.
7. **Monitoring:** Process for on-going review, progress measurement, and accountability.
8. **Exit Strategy:** Scenarios and timing triggered by strategy shifts or unsatisfactory progress.

## Specific Duties & Authorities (2013 TOR)
Based on the 1 November 2013 Terms of Reference, the EIC is granted extensive operational and evaluation scope:
- **Investment Mandate:** Determine TEG's broad policy for investment and assess candidates for external investment advisers.
- **Asset Scope:** Evaluate investments and disposals of land, campuses, buildings, and major renovations.
- **Reporting:** EIC makes recommendations to the Board of Directors of **Taylor's Education Sdn. Bhd.**, **Taylor's Education Pte Ltd**, or other operating companies.
- **Authorities:** The EIC is authorized to seek internal/external legal advice, commission independent reports or surveys, and investigate any activity within its purview.
- **Meetings:** Quorum requires at least 2 members (must include the EIC Chairman). Notice requires 7 working days.
