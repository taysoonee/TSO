# Australian International School Singapore (AISS)

## Overview
AISS is a premium international school in Singapore, part of the Cognita global network of schools. It offers an Australian curriculum alignment from Early Years through Secondary.

## 2023 Fee Structure
- **Reference:** [[AISS - 2023.pdf]]
- **Elementary (Prep to Year 5):** SGD 38,856 / year
- **Secondary (Year 6 to 9):** SGD 45,360 / year
- **Secondary (Year 10 to 12):** SGD 47,140 / year

### Upfront and Ad-Hoc Costs
- **Application Fee:** SGD 808
- **Enrolment Fee:** SGD 3,897
- **Facility Fee (Prep-Yr12):** SGD 5,427
- **Total Initial Entry Cost:** ~SGD 10,132 (approx. MYR 35,000 using SGD 1 = MYR 3.45 for historical context).
- **Payment Terms:** Half-yearly in advance, non-prorated, with a 2.5% credit card surcharge.

### Promotional Tactics
AISS employs aggressive sibling discounts primarily on upfront fees:
- **3rd Child:** 50% off Enrolment and Facility Fees, 5% off Course Fees.
- **4th+ Child:** 100% off Enrolment and Facility Fees, 10% off Course Fees.

## Strategic Impact for Taylor's Schools
The high pricing floor of AISS reinforces the overall expense of the [[Singapore K-12 Market]]. At approximately MYR 160k+ per year for Senior Secondary (plus ~MYR 35k entry), AISS inadvertently sets up a significant arbitrage play for [[Australian International School Malaysia]] (AISM). Taylor's Schools can target the regional expatriate segment facing Singapore's inflation ([[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]) by offering AISM as a highly competitive boarding alternative.

## Pricing Shift (2020)
- **Fee Increase Strategy:** For 2020, AIS implemented an average fee increase of 1.7% from Y1 to Y13. The increases were heavily weighted towards the early years: +5% for Pre-K (1/2D), +3% for Pre-K (1D) to Y6, and only +1% for Y7 to Y13.
- **Published Rates (2020):** Fees ranged from SGD 22,848 (approx. RM 79,968) for Pre-K (1/2D) to SGD 43,152 (approx. RM 151,032) for Y11-Y13.
- **Market Context:** This pricing places AIS at the upper end of the premium segment within the [[Singapore K-12 Market]], noticeably higher than the Tier 1 offerings from [[Nexus International School Singapore]].

## Update (May 14, 2026): 2019-2021 Fee Adjustments & Structuring
- **Fee Freeze (2019-2020):** After significant previous hikes, [[Australian International School Singapore]] joined [[Overseas Family School]] in holding fees flat with a 0.0% increase for the 2019-2020 cycle.
- **2020-2021 Tiered Increase:** For 2020-2021, AISS implemented a tiered fee increase averaging 1.7% (Y1 to Y13). Structurally, the hike was heavily front-loaded in the Early Years segment (+5% for Pre-K/N half-day, +3% for full-day to Y6), while intentionally shielding senior years (+1% for Y7-Y13). 
- **Strategic Impact:** This "top-heavy" pricing structure suggests a strategy to capture margin at entry points where parent demand is less elastic, while minimizing attrition risk in critical exam years. This aligns with trends monitored in the broader [[Singapore K-12 Market]].
- **Source:** [[Fees Comparison v4.3.pptx]]

## Pricing Shift (2018/19 Fee Hike)
For the 2018/19 academic year, [[Australian International School Singapore]] aggressively increased its fees by +10%, bringing its top year (Y12/G11-Y13/G12) fee to SGD 42,732 (~RM 149,562). This massive shift demonstrated high price inelasticity within the premium expat demographic, a trend closely aligned with [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]. This move pushed Taylor's Schools to model competing +3% and +8% scenarios for [[Nexus International School Singapore]]. Source: [[NISS v1.pptx]]

## Pricing Shift (2015)
- **2015 Fee Schedule:** Tuition fees per semester for AISS ranged from SGD 4,697 (Nursery) to SGD 19,594 (Secondary Year 10-12). An additional Facilities Fee of SGD 6,420 was applicable for new Elementary and Secondary students.
- **Payment Structure:** Fees were billed half-yearly in advance.
- **Market Context:** This pricing set the baseline for the premium sector in the [[Singapore K-12 Market]] a decade ago, providing a key anchor point for analyzing the subsequent compounding fee increases (e.g., 2018/19 fee hike).
- **Source:** [[AISS - 2015.pdf]]
