# Educational Technology & AI

A strategic tracking node for emerging educational technologies, pedagogical shifts, and their operational impact across K-12 institutions.

## Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Education
- **IB Policy on AI (2023):** The International Baccalaureate (IB) has officially stated that it will not ban the use of AI software like ChatGPT. Their stance is that banning is an ineffective way to handle innovation. Instead, the focus is on educating teachers and students, revising academic integrity policies (e.g., avoiding reliance on plagiarism checkers, using alternative assessments, version history), and integrating AI into learning.
- **Competitor Integration:** Regional competitors like the **International School Bangkok (ISB)** are actively integrating AI into classroom activities. Examples include "BS Detector" tasks where students critically evaluate AI-generated text for factual errors, using AI to generate basic essay frameworks for students to improve upon, and comparing student-written code with AI-generated code.

## Virtual Reality (VR) Immersive Learning
- **Pedagogical Value:** VR is increasingly used to provide realistic, engaging experiences that are otherwise impossible, unsafe, or inaccessible. A key application is developing empathy (e.g., ISB utilizing the "Clouds Over Sidra" Syrian refugee simulation for Grade 7 Social Studies).
- **Hardware and Operational Constraints:** Deployment of VR (e.g., Meta/Oculus Quest 2, costing approximately BHT 15,000 or ~MYR 1,935 per headset) faces several operational hurdles. These include student motion sickness, fragmented and developing content ecosystems, device-specific content constraints, and the logistical burden of maintaining and charging multiple headsets.
