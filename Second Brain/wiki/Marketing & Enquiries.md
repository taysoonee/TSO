## Update (May 14, 2026): Singapore Competitor Fee Aggregation (v6.7)
- **Market Stabilization (2021-2022):** The 'Fees Comparison v6.7' aggregation confirms the post-COVID stabilization of the Singapore Foreign System Schools (FSS) market. Following a universal fee freeze in 2020-21, 2021-22 saw moderate fee hikes across the board: TTS (+3.1%), SAIS (+3.8%), XCL (+2.8%), Dover (+2.9%), and NLCS (+2.0%). 
- **Aggressive Outliers:** Against this trend, [[ISS International School]] instituted a tactical -3.8% fee reduction, and [[The Perse School Singapore]] formalized its tapering discount strategy (10% off the SGD 25,000 base in 2022-23) to aggressively capture market share.
- **Strategic Impact:** The stabilization validates [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] by demonstrating renewed pricing power among top-tier legacy schools, while the aggressive undercutting by ISS and Perse highlights intense hyper-local competition and validates [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]] regarding price-sensitive segments.

## Update (May 14, 2026): High-Friction Entry Strategies & Premium Positioning
- **Competitor Baseline (Dover Court):** The 2019-2020 fee structure for [[Dover Court International School]] (Nord Anglia) demonstrates a deliberate high-friction entry strategy. Before tuition is even considered, parents face a non-refundable S$1,000 (~MYR 3,090) Application Fee and a S$3,000 (~MYR 9,270) Registration Fee. 
- **Strategic Impact:** This aggressive upfront cost structure, coupled with a mandatory S$1,500/year Building Fund, acts as a filter to ensure cohort commitment and secure high-margin early revenue. It validates the pricing power of legacy premium brands in expat hubs, contrasting sharply with the low-barrier entry tactics often seen in mid-tier or emerging K-12 markets.
