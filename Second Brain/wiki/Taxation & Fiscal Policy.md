# Taxation & Fiscal Policy

## Historical Context: GST (2015-2018)
- **Implementation Date:** 1 April 2015.
- **Standard Rate:** 6%.
- **Structure:** Zero-rated list included basic food staples (rice, fresh meat, vegetables), while processed and sundry goods were taxed.
- **Strategic Impact:** GST treatments on groceries and stationery directly influence the operational costs of school canteens and the general [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Individual Tax Reliefs (TP1)
- **Education Relief (C3):** Incentivizes self-upskilling and higher education, relevant for [[Taylor's University]] and lifelong learning paths.
- **SSPN Savings (C8):** Net savings in Skim Simpanan Pendidikan Nasional provide significant tax rebates, encouraging long-term financial planning for K-12 and tertiary education.
- **Strategic Relevance:** These reliefs are critical components in mitigating the [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]] by effectively increasing disposable income for families opting for private/international education. Taylor's Marketing can leverage these incentives to highlight the 'true cost' of education after tax benefits.

## GST Pre-Tertiary Education Guidelines (2014 Framework)
- **Exempt Supplies:** Private schools' core educational services, curriculum-related materials, direct student food/boarding, and transport are GST-exempt.
- **Input Tax Challenge:** Schools cannot claim input tax credits on acquisitions made to provide exempt supplies, causing potential margin squeeze on overheads.
- **Taxable Supplies (6%):** Non-curriculum ECAs (e.g., ballet, piano), uniforms, facility rentals, and franchised programs are standard-rated.
- **Tax Relief:** Private educational institutions can claim relief from GST payment on specific teaching goods (multimedia aids, lab equipment, vocational tools) via the First Schedule GST Relief Order 2014, requiring tight procurement oversight.
- **Strategic Impact:** This complex mixed-supply environment compounds the [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]] by forcing schools to absorb costs or pass them on via taxable non-core fees.

### 2015 Baseline Tax Relief Limits
- **Education Fees (C3):** RM5,000 limit.
- **SSPN Savings (C8):** RM6,000 limit.
- **Education/Medical Insurance (C12):** RM3,000 limit.
- **Strategic Impact:** These caps represent the 2015 baseline of governmental financial buffering for families, relevant for analyzing longitudinal shifts in parental disposable income and the [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].

## Stamp Act 1949 (Act 378 - Revised Jan 2024)
- **Overview:** The updated Stamp Act (incorporating Act 851) formalizes electronic assessments, digital stamping (stamp certificates), and updated ad valorem duty calculations.
- **Advance Duty on Immovable Property:** Section 36AA enables the Collector to assess advance duty on real property transfers. This directly impacts cash flow timing and closing costs for campus land acquisitions.
- **Corporate Restructuring Relief:** Section 15A provides stamp duty relief for the transfer of property between associated companies (e.g., intra-group real estate transfers) to achieve greater operational efficiency, subject to a 90% ownership threshold and a 3-year holding condition. 
- **Strategic Impact (Executive):** Essential regulatory parameters for K-12 capital expenditure models. Accurate projections of ad valorem duties for property conveyances (Item 32) and long-term commercial leases (Item 49) are mandatory for Greenfield/Brownfield feasibility studies, supporting the strategic imperatives of [[Brownfield Saturation Limits]] and guiding M&A exercises under [[Investment Committee]] purview.

## Education SST Implementation (2025/2026)
The Malaysian government is instituting a 6% Service Tax (SST) on annual school fees exceeding MYR 60,000, effective July/September 2025 (Malaysian students with OKU cards are exempt). This will acutely impact premium tier pricing at [[Garden International School]] and [[Nexus International School Malaysia]], intensifying the pressures identified in [[The Living Ledger]] Thesis 2 (Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze). This may drive domestic flight towards mid-premium or affordable segments like [[Lexel]].
