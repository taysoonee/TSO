# Singapore Private Education Regulations

This document outlines the key regulatory frameworks governing Private Education Institutions (PEIs) in Singapore, with a primary focus on the EduTrust Certification Scheme.

## EduTrust Certification Scheme

The EduTrust Certification Scheme is a voluntary quality assurance scheme administered by the Committee for Private Education (CPE), part of SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG). It serves as a prerequisite for PEIs wishing to enroll international students who require a Student's Pass.

It is part of a two-tier regulatory system:
1.  **Enhanced Registration Framework (ERF):** Sets the mandatory minimum standards for all PEIs to operate.
2.  **EduTrust Certification:** A mark of higher quality and a requirement for recruiting international students.

### The 7 EduTrust Criteria

The assessment for EduTrust certification is based on seven core criteria, with a total of 1,000 points distributed among them:

1.  **Leadership and Strategic Planning (60 points):** Examines the PEI's leadership, corporate governance, and strategic planning processes.
2.  **Corporate Administration (100 points):** Covers human resources, communication, data management, and feedback systems.
3.  **External Recruitment Agents (60 points):** Focuses on the selection, management, and evaluation of third-party student recruitment agents to ensure ethical practices.
4.  **Student Protection and Support Services (200 points):** A heavily weighted criterion covering pre-course counseling, student contracts, the Fee Protection Scheme (FPS), and policies for refunds, transfers, and withdrawals.
5.  **Academic Systems and Processes (200 points):** Assesses course design, delivery, student assessment, and partnerships with external academic bodies.
6.  **Quality Assurance, Innovation, and Continual Improvement (50 points):** Evaluates the systems for internal assessment, management review, and driving innovation.
7.  **Performance Outcomes (330 points):** The most significant criterion, measuring the PEI's actual results in student and graduate outcomes, service quality, operational efficiency, and staff development.

### EduTrust Award Tiers

There are three levels of certification, valid for up to four years for the top two tiers and one year for the provisional level:

-   **EduTrust Star (750+ points):** Awarded to PEIs demonstrating outstanding performance and a benchmark for the sector.
-   **EduTrust (600-749 points):** For PEIs sustaining an excellent level of performance.
-   **EduTrust Provisional (500-599 points):** For PEIs that have met the minimum level of performance and are striving for improvement.

### Strategic Implications for Market Entry & Operations
- **High Barrier to Entry:** The comprehensive nature of the 7 criteria, particularly the heavy weighting on Performance Outcomes and Student Protection, creates a significant regulatory barrier for new entrants in the [[Singapore]] market.
- **Operational Overhead:** Compliance requires robust internal systems for data management, quality assurance, and financial controls (e.g., Fee Protection Scheme), impacting operational costs.
- **Agent Management is Key:** The explicit rules under Criterion 3 place a heavy compliance burden on schools for the actions of their recruitment agents, a key channel for [[Admissions & Operations]].
- **Benchmarking Standard:** The EduTrust framework provides a clear public standard against which competitors like [[Stamford American International School]], [[Australian International School Singapore]], and [[Dulwich College Singapore]] can be benchmarked.
