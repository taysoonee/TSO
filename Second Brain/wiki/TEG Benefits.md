# TEG Benefits

Taylor's Education Group (TEG) provides a comprehensive benefits package to its employees, as detailed in the [[TEG Staff Handbook - April 2012.pdf]] and updated in the [[2019 Benefits Update - FAQ.pdf]].

## Leave Policy
### Annual Leave
- **Entitlement:** Generally 25 days for most management and executive roles as of 2019 [[2019 Benefits Update - FAQ.pdf]].
- **Carry Forward:** Effective 1 January 2020, there is no carry forward of annual leave; unutilized balance as of 31 Dec is forfeited.
- **Academic Staff:** Follow term breaks [[2019 Benefits Update - FAQ.pdf]].

### Special Leave
- **Marriage:** 3 working days (first legal marriage).
- **Paternity:** 2 working days (up to 5 children).
- **Marriage of Children:** 1 working day.
- **Compassionate Leave:** 2 working days for death of immediate family members.

## Medical Benefits
As of 2025, Taylor's Education Group has transitioned its medical coverage to **AIA Bhd.**, consisting of the **Group Health Plan (GHP)** for inpatient/surgical needs and the **Preferred Care Program** for outpatient services [[Inpatient Policy.pdf]], [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].

### Inpatient & Surgical (Group Health Plan)
Coverage is categorized into distinct plans based on employee seniority:
- **Plan 300:** For VVIP, Senior Staff, Senior Teachers, and Management.
    - **Room & Board:** RM300/day (up to 150 days).
    - **Annual Limit:** RM100,000 per annum [[Inpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Plan 200:** For Executive, Non-Executive, Junior Staff, and Teachers.
    - **Room & Board:** RM200/day (up to 150 days).
    - **Annual Limit:** RM60,000 per annum [[Inpatient Policy.pdf]].

**Key Features:**
- **Dependent Coverage:** Eligible members (including Top & Senior Management) can cover legal spouses and children (up to 19, or 23 if full-time students) under the Group Health Plan [[Inpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Surgical Coverage:** Medically necessary surgeries (e.g., fibroid removal) are fully reimbursable up to the annual limit, provided they do not fall under specific exclusions like cosmetic surgery or maternity/pregnancy-related hospitalisation [[Inpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Cashless Admission:** Available at panel hospitals with a Letter of Guarantee (LOG) [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Worldwide Coverage:** Benefits are applicable 24 hours a day worldwide, though overseas treatment is limited to local equivalent rates [[Inpatient Policy.pdf]].

### Outpatient Medical (Preferred Care Program)
Managed via the **MY AIA mobile application** (e-Member ID) [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **GP Care:** Covers routine consultations, medications, injections, and diagnostic lab/X-ray procedures at AIA Panel GP Clinics [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Specialist Care:** Requires referral from a Panel GP (except for Paediatric care for children below 12).
- **Other Benefits:** Includes Dental Care, Optical Care, and Annual Health Screenings [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].
    - **Dental Limit:** RM1,000 per annum (per employee).
    - **Optical Limit:** RM1,000 per annum (per employee).
    - **Health Screening:** RM1,000 per annum (per employee).
    - **Combined Limit:** Total annual limit for outpatient benefits (GP + Specialist + Dental + Optical + GMEX + GAS) is capped between **RM2,000 and RM4,000** depending on the specific plan [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Subsidiaries Covered:** The program extends to all major TEG entities including Taylor's University, Taylor's International Schools, GIS, AISM, and NISM [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].

### Historical Context
The "cafeteria" concept for outpatient medical was introduced in 2011 [[Medical  Healthcare Plan-1Jan11.pdf]] and refined in 2016 [[Medical & Healthcare Plan 2016 - Policy.pdf]] before the current AIA transition.

### Insurance
- **Group Hospitalisation and Surgical Insurance:** Transitioned to AIA GHP in 2025.
- **Group Personal Accident (GPA) Insurance:** 24-hour worldwide coverage, 48x monthly basic salary (min RM50,000). PREMIUM is fully paid by the Company [[Medical & Healthcare Plan 2016 - Policy.pdf]].

### Corporate Travel Insurance
Staff on official business are covered under **AIA Smart Corporate Travel Insurance**.
- **Accidental Death & Disablement:** RM500,000.
- **Medical Expenses:** Up to RM200,000 (includes post-hospitalization).
- **Emergency Medical Evacuation:** Unlimited.
- **Baggage Delay:** RM200 for every 6 hours (up to RM1,000) [[AIA - Schedule of Benefits.pdf]].

## Education Benefits
- **Free Education:** Eligible for programmes offered by Taylor's, subject to bonding (1-3 years depending on fee).
- **Children's Education:** Structured tuition fees for children of confirmed employees within TEG institutions.

## Strategic Overview (2025)
The 2025 medical insurance transition covered a significant portion of the TEG workforce:
- **Total Employees Covered:** 2,056 [[Outpatient Policy.pdf]].
- **Total Lives (incl. Dependants):** 2,362.
- **Estimated Total Premium:** ~RM 6.05M (inclusive of taxes).

## Allowances
- **Subsistence Allowance (Outstation):** RM90 (as of 2019).
- **Per Diem (Overseas):** 60-90 USD (depending on country, as of 2019).

## 2016 Benefits Revision
Effective 01 January 2016, Taylor's Education Group (TEG) implemented the following revisions to enhance staff welfare:
- **Prolonged Illness Leave:** Significantly improved to 6 months Full Pay and 6 months Half Pay (up from the previous 3m Full / 3m Half / 6m Quarter structure).
- **Paternity Leave:** Increased from 2 days to **3 days**.
- **Optical Benefits (New):** Introduced with a limit of RM200 per annum, utilized as part of the RM500 entitlement for dental and health screenings.
- **Retirement Benefits (New):** Introduced for permanent full-time staff retiring at 60:
    - **RM3,000** for 10+ years of service.
    - **RM4,500** for 20+ years of service.
    - **RM6,000** for 30+ years of service.

The **HR10-G-21** guideline (Effective 1 Jan 2011) introduced the 'cafeteria' concept to meet diverse employee needs across [[Taylor's Education Group]].
- **Outpatient Reimbursables:** General Practitioner, Specialist (with referral), and Complementary/Alternative treatments (Naturopathy, Homeopathy, Chiropractic, and Acupuncture) from MOH-recognized practitioners.
- **Outpatient Plans:** Tiers A, B, C, and D based on job grade (Top/Senior Management, Middle Management & Below, Academic).

- **Group Personal Accident:** 24-hour worldwide coverage; sum insured of 48 months basic salary (min RM50,000).
- **SOCSO Eligibility:** Compulsory for employees earning <RM3,000/month (historical threshold).
- **Strategic Context:** Part of the broader [[Admissions & Operations]] framework for maintaining institutional portfolio health and employee value proposition (EVP).

## 2019 Benefits Update Details
The following refinements were implemented in 2019 to standardize and enhance the employee value proposition across [[Taylor's Education Group]].

### Leave & WFH
- **Annual Leave Standardization:** 25 days for most staff (excluding those on term breaks).
- **Carry Forward Policy:** Terminated effective 1 January 2020. All unutilized leave must be used by year-end or forfeited.
- **Work From Home (WFH):** Formalized as a manager-approved arrangement, requiring staff to remain contactable during working hours.

### Education Bonding Structure
To support staff development, [[Taylor's Schools]] implements a bonding period for subsidized education based on course fees:
- **Course Fee >RM 20,000:** 1-year bond.
- **Course Fee RM 20,001 - RM 40,000:** 2-year bond.
- **Course Fee >RM 40,001:** 3-year bond.
- *Note: Repayment is mandatory if the employee fails or quits the course.*

### 2019 Outpatient Medical Entitlement (Pre-AIA Transition)
The entitlement was scaled based on marital status to support family welfare:
- **Top/Senior Management:** RM 3,000 (Single) / RM 4,000 (Married).
- **Middle Management:** RM 2,000 (Single) / RM 3,000 (Married).
- **Others:** RM 1,500 (Single) / RM 2,200 (Married).

### Travel & Duty Compensation (Weekend/PH)
Increased rates for staff required to work during weekends or public holidays:
- **Weekends:** RM 150 (Full day) / RM 75 (Half day).
- **Public Holidays:** RM 180 (Full day) / RM 90 (Half day).
- **Subsistence (Outstation):** RM 90 (Senior/Academic) / RM 35 (Non-Exec).
- **Per Diem (Overseas):** USD 60-90 (Country dependent).

Source: [[2019 Benefits Update - FAQ.pdf]]

### Student & Staff PA Insurance Benchmarking
As TEG expands its relationship with AIA, there is strategic value in benchmarking staff PA or conceptualizing group student insurance against AIA's retail products like the **Flex PA Plan**. The Flex PA provides targeted coverage for school-environment risks (e.g., 23 Infectious Diseases including HFMD and Dengue, Food Poisoning) and features a "Child Plus" rider (Education Allowance, Kidnap Benefit). This benchmarking aligns with broader [[Risk Management]] strategies regarding health, safety, and student welfare.
