# IGBIS (International Global School)

IGBIS is a premium IB World School located in Sierramas, Sungai Buloh.

## Admissions & Placement
- **Cutoff Date:** September 1st.
- **Grade Structure:** IB Continuum (PYP, MYP, DP, CP).
- **Placement Policy:** Uses a global grade equivalency matrix (see [[Grade Equivalency]]) to map students from UK, US, Australian, and regional Asian systems (Korea, Japan, China, Singapore).

## Strategic Positioning
- Direct competitor to [[Garden International School]] and [[Taylor's International School]] in the Klang Valley catchment.
- Strong focus on the expatriate community (Korea, Japan) which requires complex grade mapping for enrolment.

## Capacity & Facilities
- **Max Enrolment Capacity:** 1,450 students.
- **Infrastructure:** Positions as a top-tier premium school with a 540-seat theatre, indoor gym with 3 basketball courts, 50m Olympic pool, and a 400m 8-lane track. Location in Sierramas provides strategic leverage for the North West Kuala Lumpur catchment.

## Faculty Compensation & Talent Strategy (2018-2019 Baseline)
- **Salary Banding:** Gross base salaries range from MYR 152,000 to MYR 258,970 annually.
- **EPF Edge:** Employer EPF contribution is set at 15%, exceeding the statutory requirement.
- **USD Pegging:** A critical talent retention tactic allows expatriate teachers to peg up to 40% of their base salary to the USD, shielding them from MYR depreciation. This significantly advantages IGBIS in securing premium international talent compared to competitors relying solely on MYR packages.
- **Perks:** Housing allowances of MYR 3,000 - 5,000/month, and 100% tuition waivers for up to 2 children.
- **Strategic Impact:** The lucrative expatriate packages and USD hedging place upward pressure on [[Taylor's Schools]]' operational expenditure to remain competitive in acquiring top-tier educators, linking to [[Macroeconomic Outlook]] and [[Competitor Analysis]].

## FY2021 Financial Distress
- **Financial Impact:** Experienced an 18.5% YoY revenue drop to RM 24.6m, resulting in a severe RM -9.3m PBT deficit.
- **Strategic Implication:** Operating at a heavy loss places IGB in a vulnerable position within the Tier 1 segment, making them highly susceptible to broader economic shifts and potential restructuring.

## Pricing Shift & Aggressive Acquisition (2026 Update)
IGBIS has adopted aggressive discounting to poach mid-market students from competitors. New promotions include:
- A 5% annual fee discount for full payment.
- Enrolment fee waivers specifically targeting students transferring from other local international schools.
This presents a direct threat to the enrolment pipelines of [[Taylor's International School]] and [[Nexus International School Malaysia]].
