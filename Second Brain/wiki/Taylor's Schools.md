## 2014-2020 Strategic Plan KPIs & Infrastructure
Based on the 2014 Strategic Plan, the overarching 2020 Mission was "12 New Schools by Year 2020," aiming for a total of 15 schools. Key performance indicators projected an enrolment growth from 5,405 in 2013 to 8,500 by 2020, with a corresponding turnover target increase from RM 222m to RM 600m. Significant brand structuring included the rebranding of Sri Garden International to [[Taylor's International School]] KL (TISKL) in 2014, and solidifying [[Garden International School]] as a premier British school in Asia. Infrastructure expansion goals for 2015-2020 included the TIS Puchong start-up, a new campus for [[Nexus International School Singapore]], exploring an Ipoh campus, and considerations for Kuantan (initially explored as GIS Kuantan, later related to [[Project TIS Kuantan]]).

## Governance & Management Framework (SD/G/ADM/009)
- **Standardized Structure:** All schools under the division (GIS, AISM, NISP, NISS, TISKL, TISPC) follow a unified governance model.
- **Reporting Hierarchy:** 
    - Principals of most schools report directly to the **President of Taylor's Schools** ([[BK Gan]]).
    - **Nexus Exception:** Principals of NISP and NISS report to the **Head Principal of Nexus**, who then reports to the President.
- **Shared Services:** TEG Corporate Office provides enterprise-wide support in HR, Finance/Accounts, Purchasing, Payroll, Facilities, Corporate Affairs, Legal, Internal Audit, and Security.
- **Outsourced Functions:** Schools typically outsource Cafeteria, Housekeeping, Student Transport, and Bookshops.
- **The TIS Hub:** A centralized administrative unit for Taylor's International School campuses, managing non-academic operations under the leadership of the **TIS COO**.
- **Management Meetings:**
    - **Monthly Management Meeting (MMM):** Chaired by the President to review school progress, project updates, and approvals.
    - **Board of Governors (BOG):** Quarterly meetings for academic alignment and guidance per the MOE (Malaysia) or CPE (Singapore) regulations.
- **Source:** [[Admin-School Governance and Management- SD_G_ADM_009 - rev2.pdf]]

## Operations & ESG (2023): Solar System Rollout
- **Project Scope:** Implementation of a portfolio-wide Solar System across [[Garden International School]] (GIS), [[Australian International School Malaysia]] (AISM), [[Taylor's International School]] KL (TISKL), and TIS Puchong (TISPC).
- **Status (Jan 2023):** Site surveys and energy logging were successfully completed for GIS, AISM, and TISKL.
- **Operational Bottleneck:** The critical path was severely delayed by the TNB Change of Tenancy requirement (targeted Jan 30, 2023) for AISM, TISKL, and TISPC. This administrative friction threatened downstream SEDE NEM and ST Public License applications. 
- **Strategic Impact:** Connects to broader [[ESG]] risk analysis strategies (as per [[The Living Ledger]]) aiming for carbon reduction and utility cost optimisation across K-12 assets.
- **Source:** [[Progress Update.pptx]]

## Post-COVID Strategy: "Regaining our Growth Momentum" (Nov 2021)
In November 2021, the K-12 division outlined its post-COVID recovery and growth plan ("Regaining our Growth Momentum"). 
- **Financial Relief Deployed:** During the crisis, Taylor's Schools provided RM10m total via a 10% fee discount (2020), froze 2021 fees in Malaysia and Singapore, disbursed RM1.7m through Taylor's Covid-19 Financial Assistance (TCFA), and committed RM3m to Taylor's Covid Bursary (TCB) which extended into mid-2022.
- **Marketing Strategy:** Transitioned to aggressive entry-cost reduction offers and established corporate partnerships to simulate early enrolment. A division-wide Brand Equity Survey by BDRC was also commissioned.
- **Strategic Trajectory:** The executive focus shifted toward capturing opportunities driven by economic recovery, engaging "more discerning parents," and evaluating M&A opportunities involving distressed schools under Project Proventus.
- **Linkage:** [[Admissions & Operations]], [[Project Proventus]], [[Market Intelligence]]
