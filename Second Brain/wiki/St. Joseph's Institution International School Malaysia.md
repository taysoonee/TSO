## Update (May 14, 2026): Enrolment Volatility & Post-Pandemic Recovery (Project Tango)
Analysis of a confidential enrolment breakdown (Sept 2019 - Apr 2023) under the [[Project Tango]] M&A evaluation reveals the following critical pipeline intelligence:
- **Pandemic Contraction:** SJIIM's total enrolment fell sharply from 819 (Sept 2019) to a trough of 645 (Jan 2022), representing a 21% contraction primarily driven by expatriate flight and [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]].
- **V-Shaped Recovery:** The pipeline forecasts a robust recovery, returning to 804 total students by April 2023. This rapid rebound indicates strong underlying brand equity and aligns perfectly with the RM 46.44M revenue baseline observed in FY2021 (~RM 59,000 average fee).
- **Demographic Vulnerability:** The data exposed a massive collapse in the Early Years segment during COVID-19. Nursery and Reception numbers plummeted from 49 combined (Sept 2019) to just 14 (Sept 2021). 
- **Exam-Year Retention:** Conversely, exam-year students (Y10-Y13) remained highly sticky due to academic switching costs, with Year 10 actually growing from 65 to 72 over the same period. This highlights the operational resilience of senior school segments during macro shocks. 
- **Source:** [[2. Student enrolment breakdown by year.pdf]]

In August 2023, SJIIM expanded its pre-university offering by introducing **A-Levels** to run alongside its existing [[IBDP]] programme.
