# ESG Initiative: Portfolio-Wide Solar PV Integration

This page documents the strategic deployment of Solar Photovoltaic (PV) systems across the Taylor's Schools portfolio. This initiative serves as a primary operational hedge against utility inflation, directly supporting [[The Living Ledger]] Thesis 2.

## Strategic Rationale

The integration of Solar PV systems is a key component of the group's long-term financial and environmental strategy.

1.  **OPEX Stabilisation:** By generating our own electricity, we fix a significant and volatile operational cost. This mitigates the risk of sudden OPEX spikes due to national energy subsidy rationalisation or tariff increases, reducing the need for reactive, OPEX-driven fee hikes.
2.  **Competitive Advantage:** Maintaining fee stability is critical for attracting and retaining students from the price-sensitive domestic M40/T20 segments. This initiative directly supports our market positioning by ensuring long-term affordability.
3.  **ESG Branding:** The investment enhances our brand reputation as a forward-thinking, sustainable organisation. Tangible metrics, such as CO2 emissions saved, provide powerful content for marketing and stakeholder communications.

### Phase 2: Formalisation &amp; K-12 Rollout (2021-2023)

Following the baseline set by the [[Taylor's University Solar Baseline (2021)]], the K-12 portfolio rollout was systematically executed.

-   **Structural Vetting (Aug 2022):** Foundational structural endorsement and enforcement calculations were completed for GIS, TISKL, TISPC, and AISM.
-   **Confirmed Investments (2023):**
    -   [[TISPC Solar CapEx Hedge]]: RM 1.62M for 684.52 kWp.
    -   [[TISKL Solar CapEx Hedge]]: RM 493.9k for 190.75 kWp.
    -   [[AISM Solar CapEx Hedge]]: RM 675k for 263.78 kWp.
    -   [[NISM Solar CapEx Hedge]]: RM 258.8k for 89.10 kWp.
