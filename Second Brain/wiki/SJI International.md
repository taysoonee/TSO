# SJI International (St. Joseph's Institution International)

## Overview
SJI International is a prominent Catholic international school in Singapore, operated by the De La Salle Brothers. It serves as a major competitor in the premium international education segment in the region.

## 2023 Elementary School Fees
Based on the 2023 Fee Schedule, SJI International Elementary School commands ultra-premium pricing, heavily targeting the affluent expatriate demographic identified in [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].

- **Application Fee:** SGD 1,620 (~RM 5,000)
- **Enrolment Fee:** SGD 3,780 (~RM 11,667)
- **Prep 1 to Grade 3 Tuition & Development:** SGD 34,952 (~RM 107,886)
- **Grade 4 Tuition & Development:** SGD 35,036 (~RM 108,145)
- **Grade 5 to Grade 6 Tuition & Development:** SGD 35,204 (~RM 108,664)

*(Note: SGD to MYR conversion at ~3.0867 based on current baselines).*

### Structural Costs
- **Development Fee:** SGD 2,914 (~RM 8,994) charged annually for ongoing campus upkeep, integrated into the total school fees.
- **EAL/ELA Support:** An additional SGD 3,402 (~RM 10,499) annually for students requiring English language support.
- **Sibling Discount:** A 25% discount on Tuition Fees is provided for the fourth child and above.

## Strategic Implication for Taylor's Schools
The sheer cost of an elementary education at SJI (exceeding RM 108k annually) underscores the immense cost-of-living pressure on expatriates in Singapore. This dynamic creates a strong regional arbitrage opportunity for Taylor's Schools (e.g., [[Nexus International School Singapore]] or Malaysian premium campuses like [[Garden International School]]), specifically when positioning boarding and holistic value against ultra-expensive Singaporean day schools. This aligns with [[Singapore K-12 Market]] pricing ceilings.

## 2021 High School (Sec 1 to JC 2) Fees
Based on the 2021 Fee Schedule from [[SJI 2021.pdf]], SJI International's high school and IB Diploma programs establish historical premium benchmarks for the Singapore market. 

- **Application Fee:** SGD 1,605 (~RM 5,617 at 3.5x)
- **Enrolment Fee:** SGD 3,745 (~RM 13,107)
- **Security Deposit:** SGD 6,000 (~RM 21,000)
- **Development Fee (Annual):** SGD 2,676 (~RM 9,366)
- **G7-G8 (Sec 1-2) Tuition:** SGD 29,168/year (~RM 102,088)
- **G9-G10 (Sec 3-4) Tuition:** SGD 29,940/year (~RM 104,790)
- **G11-G12 (IB Diploma) Tuition:** Average SGD 34,058/year (~RM 119,203)

### Strategic Implication
These historical High School fees confirm the long-standing ultra-premium pricing in Singapore. The ~RM 119k/year cost for an IB Diploma strongly supports [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and the regional arbitrage strategy. High secondary and IB costs in Singapore provide Taylor's Schools (especially boarding campuses) a compelling angle to capture expatriate families seeking premium education at a more sustainable price point in Malaysia.

## 2014 High School (Sec 1 to JC 2) Fees
Based on the 2014 Fee Schedule from [[SJI.pdf]], historical fees demonstrate the baseline trajectory of SJI International's premium pricing.

- **Application Fee:** SGD 1,070 (~RM 3,306)
- **Registration Fee:** SGD 570 (~RM 1,761)
- **Security Deposit:** SGD 6,000 (~RM 18,540)
- **Development Fee (Annual):** SGD 2,140 (~RM 6,612)
- **G7-G8 (Sec 1-2) Tuition:** SGD 23,332/year (~RM 72,095)
- **G9-G10 (Sec 3-4) Tuition:** SGD 23,276/year (~RM 71,922)
- **G11-G12 (IB Diploma) Tuition:** Average SGD 27,248/year (~RM 84,196)

### Historical Pricing Trend
Comparing 2014 to 2021/2023, there has been consistent, aggressive inflation in Singapore's international school fees (e.g., IB Diploma tuition rising from SGD 27,248 to SGD 34,058 by 2021). This sustained fee escalation highlights the long-term structural advantage for Malaysian boarding and premium schools as a cost-effective alternative for the regional expat market, directly supporting [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and the [[Singapore K-12 Market]] pricing dynamics.
