# Overseas Family School (OFS) Singapore

## Profile
Overseas Family School (OFS) is a major international school competitor operating in Singapore. It primarily serves the expatriate community.

## Pricing & Competitor Strategy (2022-2023)
- **Fee Hikes & GST Impact:** Between Semester 1 (August 2022) and Semester 2 (January 2023), OFS implemented a mid-year tuition fee increase of approximately 6-8% across its programs. For example, High School (Grade 11-12) fees increased from S$20,500 (~RM 63,345) to S$22,200 (~RM 68,598) per semester. This increase incorporated the Singapore GST hike to 8% (effective Jan 1, 2023) alongside a base fee raise. 
- **High Entry Barriers:** The school maintains a flat Registration Fee of S$2,000 (~RM 6,180) across all grades. 
- **Strategic Impact:** The annualized High School fee at OFS reaches S$44,400 (~RM 137,000). The compounding effect of Singapore's GST hike and continuous premium fee increases heavily impacts the cost of living for expats. This directly supports the 'Education Arbitrage' narrative outlined in [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and highlights opportunities for Malaysian K-12 assets (like [[Nexus International School Malaysia]] or Taylor's boarding options) to capture price-sensitive expats relocating or seeking regional alternatives.

## Update (May 14, 2026): 2015 Financial Baseline
- **Profitability Benchmark:** In 2015, OFS operated as one of the most profitable mature campuses in the Singapore market. It generated SGD 96.6M (~MYR 336.2M) in revenue and maintained a highly lucrative 18% PBT margin, netting SGD 17.4M (~MYR 60.5M) in Profit Before Tax. 
- **Strategic Implication:** This cash generation capability highlights the extreme profitability of a scaled, mature international school, setting a baseline for regional Tier-1 FSS performance.

## Pricing Shift (2019/2020)
- **Fee Increase:** OFS implemented a +1% fee increase for the 19/20 academic year.
- **Published Rates (19/20):** Senior year fees (Y12-Y13) were set at SGD 41,000 (approx. RM 143,500). Within the [[Singapore K-12 Market]], OFS maintains a slightly lower price point compared to the Tier 1 offerings of [[Nexus International School Singapore]].

## Pricing Shift & Policy Baseline (2017)
- **Fee Baseline:** In 2017, High School (Grade 11-12) fees were S$41,000/year (~MYR 126,743 at prevailing 3.09 exchange rate) and Elementary (Grade 1-5) was S$32,500/year (~MYR 100,467). Registration fees were maintained at S$2,000 (~MYR 6,182). 
- **Open Admissions Strategy:** OFS explicitly marketed an "open entry" policy requiring no entry tests or English language proficiency prerequisites. This reveals a high-volume, low-friction acquisition funnel designed to rapidly capture the transient expatriate market without the bottleneck of selective academic screening.
- **Strategic Implication:** This establishes a historical baseline for an aggressive expat capture model. The extreme absolute costs in Singapore, coupled with basic open entry, provides Taylor's Schools (via boarding or regional appeal) a strong "Education Arbitrage" positioning lever against Singaporean competitors. Linked to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]] and [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]].
