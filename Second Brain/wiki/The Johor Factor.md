# The Johor Factor

## Cross-Border Enrollment Leakage
- **Mechanism:** The cost disparity between the [[Singapore K-12 Market]] and Malaysia's southern corridor (Johor Bahru) drives "enrollment leakage."
- **2018 Baseline:** Market intelligence from 2018 identified a growing trend of Singapore-based expats opting for Johor Bahru schools (e.g., Marlborough College Malaysia) to achieve a "Premium British" education at roughly 30% lower costs, despite the cross-border commute.
- **Strategic Impact:** As Singaporean Tier-1 fees exceed SGD 45,000 (MYR 157,000), boarding or commuting to Johor becomes a highly viable alternative for cost-conscious, "Local-Plus" expatriates. This validates the structural relevance of the region for Taylor's Schools' regional hub strategy and [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].

## Iskandar Expansion (2012-2014)
The 2012-2014 period saw a significant influx of premium international brands into Johor, driven by the Iskandar development mandate. Key entrants included [[Marlborough College Malaysia]] (2012) and [[Raffles American School]] (2012/2014). This established Johor as a secondary hub for premium boarding, supporting [[Thesis 3: Boarding as a Premium Utility]].
