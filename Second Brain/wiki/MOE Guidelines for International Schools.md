# MOE Guidelines for International Schools

The establishment and registration of international schools in Malaysia are governed by the Ministry of Education (MOE) / Kementerian Pendidikan Malaysia (KPM).

## Key Regulatory Requirements (Updated Jan 2018)

### Corporate & Ownership
- **Company Structure:** Must be incorporated in Malaysia and registered with the Companies Commission of Malaysia (SSM). Education activities must be explicitly stated in the Memorandum and Articles of Association (M&A).
- **Capital:** Minimum paid-up capital of **RM 1,000,000**.
- **Equity:** 100% foreign equity ownership is permitted under the Autonomous Trade Liberalization Policy (since 31 March 2012).
- **Governance:** The Board of Directors and Board of Governors (minimum 5 members) must each include at least one (1) Malaysian citizen.

### Facilities & Premises
- **Minimum Built-up Area:**
    - Primary School: 30,000 sq ft.
    - Secondary School: 25,000 sq ft.
    - Combined (Primary + Secondary): 50,000 sq ft.
- **Capacity:**
    - Minimum Classrooms: Primary (20), Secondary (15), Combined (35).
    - Student-to-Class Ratio: 1:25.
- **Restrictions:** Temporary operations in shop lots, residential houses, or offices are prohibited.

### Curriculum & Instruction
- **Curriculum:** Must use a foreign curriculum (e.g., British, American, Australian, Canadian, or International Baccalaureate).
- **Language:** English must be the medium of instruction.
- **Accreditation:** Must obtain accreditation from an international body within three (3) years of operation.
- **Islamic Education:** Mandatory for Muslim students (if 5 or more are enrolled), requiring at least two (2) hours of instruction per week during regular hours.

### Student Composition & Requirements
- **Malaysian Students:** No quota on local student enrollment (policy effective 1 July 2012).
- **Mandatory Subjects (for Malaysians):**
    - Bahasa Melayu
    - Pendidikan Islam / Pendidikan Moral
    - Sejarah Malaysia
- **International Students:** Require approval from the Ministry of Home Affairs (KDN) and valid student passes from the Immigration Department.

### Human Capital
- **Principal/Guru Besar:** Must have professional qualifications and experience. Prohibited from managing more than one school concurrently.
- **Teachers:**
    - Encouraged to hire Malaysian citizens.
    - Foreign teachers must have a bachelor's degree and professional teaching qualifications.
    - All teachers must possess a valid **Teaching Permit**.

### Financial Governance
- **Fee Approval:** All tuition and related fees must be approved by the Registrar General prior to implementation.
- **Fee Increases:** Maximum 30% increase allowed every three (3) years.
- **Prohibitions:** Collection of development or building fees from students is strictly prohibited.
- **Deposits:** Refundable deposits should not exceed RM 5,000.

### Market Entry & Reporting
- **Market Study:** A mandatory market study must be submitted with new applications, covering demand for international education (local and expat), competitor benchmarking, and fee comparisons.
- **Data Reporting:** Schools must update data in the Private Education Institution Information System (SMIPS) three times annually (Jan, June, Oct).

## Additional Regulatory Details (from Internal Guidelines)

### Land & Capacity
- **Minimum Land Area:** Five (5) acres.
- **Target Capacity:** Building/premise must be suitable for at least 1,000 students.
- **Location Strategy:** MOE prioritizes locations with high expatriate density or within new economic development corridors.

### Governance & Administration
- **Principal Role:** Automatically serves as the Secretary to the Board of Governors. Must be present full-time and cannot serve more than one school.
- **Bumiputera Representation:** A representative of Bumiputera interests must be included in the Board of Governors.

### Market Entry Restrictions
- **Refusal Clause (Section 84(d)):** The Registrar General reserves the right to refuse registration if the proposed area already has sufficient educational facilities. This serves as a significant barrier to entry in saturated markets.
- **Advertising Ban:** Promotional activities (advertisements, brochures) are strictly prohibited until the school is fully registered or has a temporary certificate of registration.

### Strategic Implications for Taylor's Schools
- **Barrier to Entry:** The 'sufficient facilities' clause (Section 84(d)) protects established players like [[Taylor's International School]] and [[Garden International School]] but also complicates new branch expansions in competitive hubs.
- **Domestic Pivot:** The abolition of local student quotas (Circular 1/2012) validates [[T20 Inflation Squeeze]], as domestic students now form a core revenue pillar.

## International Student Recruitment (KDN Permit Lifecycle)
- **Mechanism:** International schools must obtain specific approval from the Ministry of Home Affairs (KDN) to recruit international students, following support from the Ministry of Education (MOE).
- **Validity Cycle:** Standard KDN approvals for recruitment are typically issued for a **two (2) year period** (e.g., [[St. Joseph's Institution International School Malaysia]] 2023-2025 approval).
- **Prerequisites:** Valid MOE registration (Certificate No.) and a curriculum support letter from MOE (e.g., KPM.600-8/22/122 series).
- **Strategic Impact:** The 24-month renewal cycle creates periodic regulatory 'choke points'. Failure to renew in time results in an immediate freeze on international student visas, impacting top-line enrolment for schools heavily exposed to [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]]. Monitoring competitor renewal dates (typically Q1/Q3) provides foresight into potential administrative friction for rival schools.
- **Source Case:** [[010623-dd-1.4 KDN MOH approval letter 20.3.2023.pdf]]

## Historical Policy Shifts (Market Landscape 2017)
- **2011:** Deregulation of tuition fees proposed in ETP annual report.
- **2012:** Removal of quotas for Malaysian students in international schools. Prior to 2007, Malaysians were not allowed; 2007-2012 capped at 40%.
- **2017 Baseline:** 127 international schools in Malaysia, exceeding the original EPP3 target of 87.
- **Strategic Impact:** These shifts fueled the [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]] by allowing the domestic market to become the primary growth driver for premium and mid-tier schools.

## Curriculum Mandates (Historical Verification)
- **Language Requirements (2011):** All Year 1 and 2 pupils in international schools are required by local law to receive lessons in Malay Bahasa. This historical requirement (verified in [[Alice Smith International School]] 2011 Inspection) underscores the long-standing regulatory integration of national language education within foreign curriculum frameworks.
