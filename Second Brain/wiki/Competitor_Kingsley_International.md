# Competitor Profile: Kingsley International

## Operational Status & Financial Health
Kingsley International Sdn. Bhd., a wholly-owned subsidiary of Kingsley Edugroup Berhad, illustrates the capital-intensive nature of greenfield projects observed in [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]]. Initial operations in the 2011-2012 cycle recorded a net loss of RM 1.3 million against RM 7.4 million in revenue, contemporaneous with the onboarding of a massive RM 108.5 million construction contract alongside B&G Corporation Sdn. Bhd. for their new campus. This phase was financed via a RM 76.5 million term loan secured by land, assets, and corporate guarantees.

By 2019, Kingsley effectively stabilised its operations, reporting revenues of RM 28.8 million and a net profit of RM 6.1 million. Their fiscal control is evidenced by an improving gearing ratio, reduced from 1.28 in 2018 to 1.13 in 2019, whilst actively managing legacy debt.

## Strategic Intersections
*   **Property Developer Synergy:** Extensive related-party transactions (e.g., with Kingsley Hills Sdn. Bhd. for construction costs) reflect the standard industry model of leveraging property arms to offset standalone educational capital expenditure risks.
*   **Monitoring Status:** Validates the timeline to profitability for large-scale campuses; mapped to [[Competitor Analysis]].
