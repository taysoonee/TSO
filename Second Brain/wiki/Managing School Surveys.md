# Managing School Surveys (TSO)

This document outlines the standard operating procedures, roles, timelines, and metrics for managing school surveys across Taylor's Schools. It is ingested from Google Doc `SD/OP/MKT/001 Draft`.

## 1. Core Purpose
Surveys drive excellence and compliance across four pillars:
*   **Strategic Growth**: Gathering stakeholder feedback to shape school development plans.
*   **Risk Management**: Measuring satisfaction levels and identifying operational risks early.
*   **Accreditation**: Aligning with quality tracking standards (e.g., CIS, EduTrust, SSG).
*   **Data Governance**: Ensuring strict PDPA compliance and stakeholder data privacy.

---

## 2. Key Stakeholders & Target Metrics
Surveys are targeted at four primary groups, each with specific participation benchmarks required for statistical relevance:

| Stakeholder Group | Target Participation Rate | Deployment Channel / Mobilization Method |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| **Parents** | **> 30%** | Automated notifications, school invitations, and physical on-the-ground kiosks during Parent-Teacher Conferences, Coffee Mornings, etc. |
| **Students** | **> 80%** | Administered digitally in school. Teachers allocate specific slots (e.g., ICT class or tutor time). |
| **Staff** | **> 60%** | Administered, tracked, and deployed separately by the People & Culture (P&C) team from TSO. |
| **Bus / Boarding** | *N/A* | Disseminated via targeted digital communications or localized channels managed by the School Survey Coordinator. |

---

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

### **School Principal (School Owner)**
*   **Mobilization**: Drives participation on campus, instructing teachers to run direct parent-outreach campaigns.
*   **Results Diagnosis**: Independently reviews the Looker Studio dashboard to isolate key wins and critical risks.
*   **Accountability**: Tables survey findings at Governance/Board meetings. Accountable to the CGO and COO for submitting the **School Improvement Plan** within **14 days** of receiving the survey dashboard link.

### **School Survey Coordinator (School Executor)**
*   **Milestone Ownership**: Manages local launch schedules, reminders, and deadlines based on the master calendar.
*   **Template Alignment**: Builds online forms using the corporate standardized question lists. Proposed edits or additions must be submitted through the TSO Analyst & Coordinator for CGO approval.
*   **Form Controls**: Triggers the opening and closing of forms by emailing the TSO Analyst & Coordinator (requires Principal's sign-off).
*   **Action Planning**: Convenes the SLT and operational heads (Facilities, Admissions, Student Services) to draft the mandatory Improvement Plan.

### **TSO Analyst & Coordinator (Group Oversight)**
*   **Master Planning**: Serves as the single point of oversight for the corporate office, ensuring timelines and standards are met.
*   **Gatekeeping**: Freezes master templates, rejecting unauthorized changes to maintain benchmarking capabilities and dashboard schemas.
*   **Change Control**: Processes mandatory question adjustments through the CGO and COO.
*   **Integrity Auditing**: Audits compliance with PDPA, CIS domains, and EduTrust guidelines.
*   **Pipeline Management**: Controls the opening/closing of digital forms and refreshes the automated data pipelines routing dashboards directly to Principals.

## 4. Timelines & Workflows

### **Annual Survey Timeline (14-Week Lifecycle)**
*   **Weeks 1–2**: Design and review questions; create the survey online.
*   **Weeks 3–4**: Finalize survey questions and get approval from the School Principal.
*   **Weeks 5–6**: **Start Survey (Open)**. Duration is typically 1-2 weeks (adjusted locally to hit target participation metrics).
*   **Week 7**: **End Survey (Close)** once targets are hit.
*   **Weeks 8–9**: Report preparation and processing.
*   **Weeks 10–11**: Analysis and synthesis of results.
*   **Week 12**: Present the survey report in Governance/Board of Governors (BOG) Meetings.
*   **Weeks 13–14**: Share results back with respondents (closing the loop).

### **New Parent Survey Timeline (6-Week Lifecycle)**
*   **Week 1**: Design and review questions.
*   **Week 2**: Finalize questions and secure Principal approval.
*   **Week 3**: **Start Survey (Open)**.
*   **Week 4**: **End Survey (Close)**.
*   **Week 5**: Report preparation and analysis.
*   **Week 6**: Present in Governance Meetings and communicate results.

## 5. Performance Benchmarks & School Improvement Plans

*   **Satisfaction Benchmark**: The general target for school satisfaction is **>= 70%** (calculated as total *Agree* and *Strongly Agree* responses on a 5-level Likert scale).
*   **Mandatory Action Trigger**: A School Principal **must** create a **School Improvement Plan** if:
    1.  The satisfaction score falls below the set target in any category.
    2.  For the school's **top three (3) lowest-scoring areas** (lowlights).
*   **Improvement Plan Requirements**: The plan must outline:
    *   The root cause of the low score.
    *   Specific action steps to address the issue.
    *   The designated owner for the fix.
    *   Target completion dates.
*   **Submission Deadline**: Must be submitted to the TSO corporate office (CGO/COO) within **14 days** of receiving the survey dashboard link.
*   **Tracking**: Progress on actions is monitored during subsequent Governance or Board of Governors (BOG) reviews.

---

## 6. Closing the Loop (Sharing Results)
Once finalized, a summary must be shared back with the community:
*   **Parents & Students**: Summary shared digitally via the official School newsletter.
*   **Staff**: Shared via an internal leadership memo.
*   **Content**: Must cover the biggest wins (highlights), key areas of improvement (lowlights), and the core points of the School Improvement Plan.

---

## 7. Data Archiving & Governance
*   Immediately upon closure, the TSO Survey Analyst & Coordinator will save a master backup of raw responses for IT compliance, audit protocols, and data security.
*   Question structures are aligned to the **Council of International Schools (CIS)** accreditation domains:
    *   *A. School Guiding Statements*
    *   *B. Teaching and Learning*
    *   *C. Governance and Leadership*
    *   *D. Faculty and Support Staff*
    *   *E. Access to Teaching and Learning*
    *   *F. School Culture and Partnerships for Learning*
    *   *G. Operational Systems*
