# Taylor's Schools Expansion History

## Strategic Evolution & Domestic Pivot
The institutional expansion of the Taylor's Schools K-12 portfolio reflects a sustained, systematic integration into both expatriate and domestic premium education markets. A definitive operational catalyst was the 2012 abolishment of the 40% local student quota by the authorities. This regulatory transformation propelled a rapid capitalisation on English-medium domestic demand, permanently establishing [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]] as a governing dynamic for sustained domestic enrolment.

## Implementation Timeline
The execution of the strategic roadmap illustrates long-term deployment strategies aligning with [[Thesis 4: Greenfield/Brownfield Saturation Limits]]:
- **2006:** Strategic acquisition of the [[Australian International School Malaysia]] (AISM), establishing immediate operational capacity in the Southern Corridor.
- **2007:** Greenfield establishment of [[Nexus International School Malaysia]] (NISM), driving boarding and progressive learning frameworks.
- **2011:** Regional M&A expansion via the acquisition of [[Nexus International School Singapore]] (NISS), strategically capturing affluent expatriate verticals in a high-demand market.
- **2015:** Tactical launch of [[Taylor's International School Puchong]] (TISPC), designed to scale international pedagogy for the expanding domestic middle-tier.
- **2020:** Structural relocation and campus elevation of [[Nexus International School Singapore]] to the advanced Aljunied flagship facility, fundamentally optimising metropolitan capacity and infrastructure integration.
