# Macroeconomic Outlook

## Domestic Financial Constraints
Central to [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]] is the structural downward pressure exerted on domestic discretionary income.
- **Historical Vulnerability:** Essential baseline intelligence indicates severe structural income clustering, underscoring the fundamental financial sensitivity of the majority demographic interacting with premium private education fees.
- **Inflationary Erosion:** Post-pandemic economic growth metrics must be heavily discounted against concurrent surges in core inflation and the rationalisation of state subsidies. These pressures effectively neutralise discretionary income expansion, amplifying household sensitivity to unhedged tuition escalations.

## Expatriate Dynamics & Regional Inflows
The expatriate demographic has demonstrated key realignments conducive to stability, directly interacting with [[Thesis 1: The Expatriate & FDI Inflow Vector]].
- **Cohort Stabilisation:** Long-cycle analysis of established international campuses reveals a pronounced deceleration in relocation-driven student withdrawals, indicating a measurable reduction in expatriate mobility risk.
- **FDI Alignment:** Continuous regional field monitoring confirms substantive realignments towards Asian wealth inflows. Demographic composition intelligence highlights critical concentrations of incoming Mainland Chinese enrolments, representing a strategic shift from legacy western expatriation to targeted regional investment corridors.
