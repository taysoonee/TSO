# Solar PV Integration Project

## Operational Objective
The enterprise-wide rollout of Solar PV infrastructure constitutes a critical operational hedge against structural utility inflation and the progressive rationalisation of national energy subsidies. This strategic initiative directly addresses the vulnerabilities outlined in [[Thesis 2: Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze]]. By fundamentally stabilising energy operational expenditure (OPEX), Taylor's Schools mitigates the necessity for aggressive, inflation-driven fee increments, thereby sustaining market accessibility for the price-sensitive M40 and lower-T20 domestic segments.

## Vulnerability Assessment
Granular energy consumption audits confirmed that institutional facilities, alongside comprehensive boarding and staff accommodations, consistently breached peak utility tariff brackets (>900 kWh) during standard operational cycles. To neutralise this financial vulnerability, structural endorsement and viability computations were systematically executed across the portfolio during 2022.

## Implementation Timeline & Capacity Rollout
The strategic infrastructure rollout sequenced from higher education through the K-12 campuses, ensuring long-term macro-resilience:
- **Phase 1: Baseline Establishment (2021)**
  - Taylor's University (Subang Jaya): 701.55 kWp capacity.
- **Phase 2: Core K-12 Escalation (2023)**
  - Taylor's International School Kuala Lumpur (TISKL): 190.75 kWp capacity (Implemented January 2023).
  - Australian International School Malaysia (AISM): 263.78 kWp capacity.
  - Taylor's International School Puchong (TISPC): 684.52 kWp capacity.
  - Nexus International School Malaysia (NISM): 89.10 kWp capacity (Implemented June 2023).
