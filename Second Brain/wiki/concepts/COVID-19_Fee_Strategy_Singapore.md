# COVID-19 Fee Strategy (Singapore)

During the 2020-2021 academic year, in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the Singapore Foreign System School (FSS) market saw a widespread fee freeze (0% increase) among premium competitors, including [[Singapore American School]], [[United World College (Singapore)]], [[Tanglin Trust School]], and [[Dulwich College Singapore]].

## NISS Strategic Response
[[Nexus International School Singapore]] (NISS) employed a dual-pricing strategy to defend enrolment and protect long-term published rates:
- **Published Fee:** Increased by 2.2%.
- **Ulu Pandan Discount:** Applied a 2.6% loyalty discount to the published fee for existing families.
- **Effective Increase:** 0%, aligning with the market-wide fee freeze.

## Strategic Impact
This strategy allowed NISS to defend its long-term published fee trajectory while providing immediate financial relief to families during the pandemic and its simultaneous Aljunied campus relocation.

## Personal Impact
For the Family Office, this illustrates the importance of granular, flexible pricing (yield management) as a defense mechanism to maintain enrolment volumes during macroeconomic shocks. Maintaining published rates while offering targeted loyalty discounts is superior to lowering published rates, as it avoids permanent damage to the brand's premium positioning.
