# Project TIS Kuantan

In November 2016, Taylor's Schools proposed to the **[[Investment Committee]]** to not proceed with the establishment of a TIS model school in Kuantan.

## Strategic Context
The project was initially endorsed in Gate 1 (March 2015). However, Gate 2 endorsement was withheld in December 2015 as the committee requested a shorter payback period and lower total investment.

## Key Findings & Decision
- **Market Slowdown:** Enrolment at the existing Garden International School (GIS) Kuantan school dropped by 40% (40 students) in April 2016.
- **Financial Viability:** The project was deemed not financially viable due to the slowdown in market demand.
- **Previously Proposed Scenario:** 
    - Total Investment: RM47.5 million (Land RM11m, Building RM36.4m).
    - Capacity: 332 (Phase 1), 472 (Phase 2).
    - Breakeven: 265 students (Year 4).
    - Payback Period: 13.5 years.
    - IRR: 10.4%.
- **Outcome:** Recommendation to forgo the Kuantan market for the TIS model.

### Pre-2015 Growth Phase
Before the 2016 strategic withdrawal, **GIS Kuantan** underwent a significant expansion and extension to Year 9 (2012-2013). This phase involved securing a new site lease and MOE approval, indicating a period of planned growth that preceded the eventual market saturation and slowdown.
- **Project Reference:** [[Garden International School]] Kuantan Expansion (2012-2013).
- **Source:** [[2 Page - sample GIS Kuantan Schedule copy.xls]]

### Strategic Context Update (2012 Baseline)
The 2012 expansion was driven by a robust corporate pipeline in the Kuantan industrial corridor, including significant interest from Volkswagen, DRB Hi Com, BP Chemical, Isuzu, and Haliburton. The shift to Year 9 was a defensive move against [[International School Kuantan]] (ISK). This historical context highlights the volatility of the Kuantan market, which eventually saw a 40% enrolment drop by 2016, leading to the decision to not proceed with the TIS model.
- **Source:** [[2 Page - sample GIS Kuantan 2012 Nov copy.DOC]]

## Taylor's Schools Strategic Plan (2016-2025)

The [[Taylor's Schools Strategic Plan 2016-2025]] outlined a critical decision point for the Kuantan campus. A total of **RM36 million in CAPEX** was allocated, with the final strategy pending a decision to either:
- Build a **New Campus** (under either GIS or TIS branding).
- **Divest** the asset.

This allocation was part of a broader portfolio review to determine the most financially viable path for the Kuantan market, which ultimately led to the decision not to proceed with a new TIS model campus.
