# Market Intelligence Brief: 07 June 2026

**SUBJECT: Confirmation of Systematic ESG/OpEx Hedging Strategy (Solar PV Initiative)**

## 1. Executive Summary

We have confirmed that the group's multi-million ringgit Solar PV CapEx deployment across key campuses in 2023 was preceded by systematic structural due diligence in August 2022. This foresight demonstrates a deliberate, portfolio-wide strategy to hedge against operational expenditure (OpEx) volatility, primarily driven by rising energy costs.

This initiative directly addresses **Thesis 2 (Domestic M40/T20 Inflation Squeeze)** by creating a long-term buffer against utility inflation. This strengthens EBITDA resilience and provides crucial pricing stability, which is a significant competitive advantage in a market where domestic disposable income is under pressure. This proactive investment de-risks future budgets and reinforces our commitment to operational excellence and sustainability.

## 2. Operations &amp; Admissions Brief

The August 2022 structural assessments for Australian International School Malaysia, Taylor's International School KL &amp; Puchong, and Garden International School were the foundational step for our group-wide energy security project.

- **Operational Impact:** This initiative provides long-term predictability for a major OpEx variable. By fixing a significant portion of our energy costs, campus budgets are better insulated from national subsidy rationalisation or tariff hikes. This allows for more confident long-range financial planning and resource allocation.
- **Admissions Impact:** Stable operational costs reduce the need for reactive, inflation-driven fee increases. This is critical for maintaining student retention and mitigating funnel leakage among price-sensitive domestic families. Predictable fee structures strengthen parent confidence and reduce onboarding friction.

## 3. Marketing Brief

The group-wide solar initiative offers a powerful, multi-faceted narrative that directly supports our premium brand positioning.

- **Competitive Differentiation:** We can proactively market our fee stability as a key differentiator. While competitors may be forced to pass on rising utility costs, we can highlight our investment in cost control, reinforcing our value proposition to both domestic and expatriate parents.
- **ESG Narrative:** The investment provides tangible proof of our commitment to sustainability and corporate responsibility. This is an increasingly important decision factor for the UHNW/HNWI and corporate expatriate segments. We should leverage this in brand campaigns, campus tours, and corporate partnership discussions.
- **Target Audience Resonance:** For the domestic T20/M40 segment, the message is one of prudent financial management and long-term value. For the expatriate segment, it aligns with global ESG standards. This single initiative strengthens our brand positioning across key target demographics.
